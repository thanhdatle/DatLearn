The Vocabulary

Every term defined once, and used consistently in every lesson. D&D's nomenclature is the real barrier to entry for a DOS2 player — not the tactics. Learn twenty words and the game becomes legible.

The two rolls

d20 the die
A twenty-sided die. Nearly everything uncertain in BG3 resolves by rolling one and adding modifiers. A natural 20 always succeeds; a natural 1 always fails, regardless of bonuses.wiki
Attack roll you roll
You trying to hit. d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus, must meet or beat the target's Armour Class.wiki A high roll is good for you.
Saving throw they roll
They trying to resist. The defender rolls d20 + their ability modifier against your DC. Every control spell in the game resolves this way.wiki A high roll is good for them. This inversion catches everyone.
Difficulty Class (DC) the bar
The number a roll must meet or beat. Your spell save DC is 8 + proficiency bonus + spellcasting ability modifier.wiki Raising it makes every control spell you own harder to resist.
Armour Class (AC) not a bar
A difficulty to hit, not a pool to deplete. 10 + DEX modifier + armour + shield.wiki It never decreases from being attacked, and — critically — it does nothing against saving throws. Nothing like DOS2's armour bars.
Advantage / Disadvantage two dice
Roll two d20s and take the higher (advantage) or the lower (disadvantage). It does not stack — five sources are the same as one — and one of each cancels out entirely, back to a single die.wiki Roughly worth +3 to +5 on average. The most valuable currency in the game.
Proficiency bonus scales
+2 at levels 1–4, +3 at 5–8, +4 at 9+.wiki Added to things you are trained in — weapons, skills, some saves — and to your spell save DC.
Proficiency the trap
Being trained with a weapon or armour type. Wearing armour you lack proficiency in gives disadvantage on every attack, check and save — and blocks spellcasting entirely.wiki Do not put plate on your Wizard.

The turn

Action 1 / turn
Your one big thing: Attack, cast a levelled spell, Dash, Throw. Cannot be split or saved.
Bonus Action 1 / turn
A second, smaller thing you get free every turn, with no DOS2 equivalent — which is exactly why you will forget it. Shove, drink a potion, jump, off-hand attack, dip a weapon, hide (Rogues).wiki It does not convert to anything and it does not carry over.
Movement a pool
Roughly 9 m per turn, spendable in pieces before and after your Action. Refreshes fully each turn.
Reaction 1 / round
Fires on a trigger, usually during someone else's turn. Opportunity Attack, Shield, Counterspell.wiki Configure each one to fire automatically or to Ask, in the Reactions tab of your spellbook.
Opportunity Attack reaction
A free swing at an enemy who walks out of your melee reach. Jumping away does not trigger it — which is the whole reason Jump matters.
Initiative 1d4 + DEX
Turn order. Not a d20 — BG3 deliberately uses a d4.wiki Because the die only spans 1–4, flat bonuses dominate: the Alert feat's +5 is larger than the entire range of the roll.
Surprise round free turn
Start a fight unnoticed and the attackers get a round in which only they may act.wiki DOS2's round-robin order makes this impossible; here it is the strongest opening in the game.

Casting

Concentration one at a time
Many buffs and control spells require it. One per caster — casting a second concentration spell immediately ends the first. Taking damage forces a Constitution save at DC 10, or half the damage taken, whichever is higher.wiki
The direct killer of DOS2's stack-every-buff habit.
Spell slot the fuel
Consumed to cast a levelled spell. Restored on a long rest — except Warlock slots, which come back on a short rest.wiki Max spell level in BG3 is 6.
Cantrip free forever
A level-0 spell, castable at will, costing no slot. Damage cantrips gain an extra die at character level 5 and again at 10.wiki
Prepared vs Known reversible?
Prepared casters (Cleric, Druid, Wizard, Paladin) re-pick their active spells freely outside combat — mistakes are reversible. Known casters (Sorcerer, Bard, Warlock, Ranger) learn a fixed list and swap only one spell per level.wiki Prepared classes are more forgiving on a first playthrough. Inference

Character

Ability modifier the maths
floor((score − 10) / 2). So 14 → +2, 16 → +3, 20 → +5, and 8 → −1.wiki Odd scores round down — a 17 gives the same +3 as a 16, which is why you push to even numbers.
Point buy 27 points
Character creation budget. Scores run 8–15 (8 is free), then a +2 and a +1 are applied, so you can start at 17.wiki
Feat levels 4, 8, 12
A power pick, taken at levels 4, 8 and 12 (Fighters also at 6, Rogues at 10).wiki Ability Improvement (+2 to one stat, cap 20) is itself a feat. With a level cap of 12, most characters get exactly three.
Level cap 12
The maximum character level.wiki This is why multiclassing has a real cost — every dipped level is one your main class never gets.
Withers 100 gold
The undead fellow in your camp. Rebuilds any character completely — class, stats, spells, feats — for 100 gold.wiki Your build is never permanent. Stop agonising.

Resting

Short rest 2 per long rest
Free, no supplies, no travel. Restores up to half your max HP plus Warlock slots, Ki, Channel Divinity, Action Surge, Second Wind, weapon actions.wiki You are not using these.
Long rest 40 supplies
Costs 40 camp supplies on Balanced. Restores everything, including both short rests.wiki There is no penalty for resting often, and companion scenes fire here — under-resting means missing content. (Hedge: a few side quests are rest-sensitive; no source lists which.)
