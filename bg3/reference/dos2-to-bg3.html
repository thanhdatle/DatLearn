DOS2 → BG3: What Transfers, What Betrays You

Same studio, different rulebook. Your DOS2 instincts are an asset in roughly half of BG3 and a liability in the other half — and the game will never tell you which half you are in. This table tells you. Ordered by how badly each habit misfires.

How to read the evidence badges

Mechanic — verifiable in the datamined game data. A fact.
Consensus — what good players believe. Defensible, not proven. Argue with it.
Inference — my own reasoning from the mechanics. Flagged as mine, not as truth.

The four that will actively hurt you

SystemDOS2 (your reflex)BG3 (the rule)What to do
Armour & CC
Mechanic		 Two armour bars gate all CC. Strip the bar, then CC lands at 100%.src No gate, and no bar. AC is a difficulty to hit, never a pool, and it is irrelevant to CC. Control resolves on a saving throw.wiki Lead with the control. Turn one, full-health enemy. Stop softening things up.
Buff stacking
Mechanic		 Stack every buff on the frontliner. They all persist. No limit. Concentration. One concentration spell per caster — casting a second cancels the first. Damage forces a CON save to keep it.wiki Spread buffs across different casters. Protect the concentrating one.
Action economy
Mechanic		 4 AP, one fungible pool, and leftovers roll over to next turn.src Three separate boxes: 1 Action, 1 Bonus Action, Movement. No conversion, no carry-over.wiki Check the bonus action before every End Turn. You will forget it. Everyone does.
Turn order
Mechanic		 Round-robin: ally, enemy, ally, enemy. Back-to-back ally turns are impossible.src Individual initiative (1d4 + DEX). Allies can act consecutively, and an unnoticed party gets a surprise round the enemy cannot answer.wiki Alpha strike. The thing DOS2 said was impossible is BG3's best opening. Sneak first, then talk.

The rest of the diff

SystemDOS2BG3Verdict
Surfaces Central. Often the win condition. CC from surfaces is automatic. Real and damaging, but the CC is save-gated too — Grease requires a DEX save to knock prone.wiki Demoted from win condition to a tool. Still good. No longer the plan.
Damage types Physical/magical split. Mixing is believed to be punished — and this is contested even among DOS2 players. One AC. No split exists. A hybrid party carries zero structural penalty.wiki Forget the whole concept. Build any party you like.
Initiative stat Wits. Every point of Wits = +1 initiative.src Dexterity, on a 1d4. There is no Wits stat.wiki The die only spans 1–4, so flat bonuses dominate. Alert's +5 outweighs the entire roll.
Respec Magic Mirror. Free, unlimited. Withers, 100 gold, in your camp. Resets class, subclass, stats, spells, feats.wiki Trivially cheap by mid-Act 1. Your freedom to experiment survives.
Resting No real equivalent tier. Two short rests per long rest (free, restore half HP + class resources); long rest costs 40 camp supplies.wiki You are under-resting. See the field manual — this is the most common new-player error.
Equipment swapping Cannot change gear in combat. You can carry melee and ranged and use either. Consensus Give every melee character a bow. There is no reason not to.
Dialogue De-emphasised as a build axis. A real build axis. Persuasion/Deception checks are d20 rolls you can fail. Consensus Have one face character with Charisma and lead conversations with them.
Friendly fire Your AoE will absolutely kill your own party. Same. Surfaces and AoE hit allies. Inference This one transfers intact. Keep spreading out. Your instincts are correct here.

What transfers, untouched

Half your DOS2 brain is still an asset. Do not throw out the following — they are as true in Faerûn as they were on Reaper's Coast: positioning and elevation matter (though in BG3 high ground is a flat +2, not advantagewiki); spread out against AoE; scout before you commit; the environment is a weapon (explosive barrels work exactly as you would hope); quicksave constantly; and talk to everything before you fight it.

Three myths to unlearn immediately

You will be told…The truth Mechanic
"High ground gives advantage"Flat +2 to attack rolls; low ground is −2. Useful, but far weaker than advantage.wiki
"Backstabbing gives advantage"It gives nothing. Removed after Early Access; absent from the advantage list.wiki
"Initiative is a d20"1d4 + DEX. Tabletop uses d20; BG3 deliberately does not.wiki
One open question, stated honestly

bg3.wiki lists Prone as a condition that breaks concentration.wiki That contradicts tabletop 5e, where it does not — so it may be a wiki error. If it is true it matters a great deal to you specifically: the Grease and Ice surfaces a DOS2 player reaches for by instinct knock creatures prone, and could therefore break your own team's concentration. Treat as unconfirmed and watch for it in-game. I would rather flag this than quietly smooth it over.
