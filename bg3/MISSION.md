# Mission: Baldur's Gate 3

## Why

Finish a first playthrough of Baldur's Gate 3 on Balanced without ever hitting a wall — no
savescumming every fight, no panicked respec at level 7, no wiki open on the second monitor.
The real goal is **competence, not optimisation**: making good decisions in the moment because
the system is understood, not because a guide was consulted.

There is a specific asset and a specific liability. Dat has played **Divinity: Original Sin 2**
to completion. That means the Larian *interface* — turn-based tactics, environmental thinking,
party management, quicksave discipline — is already fluent. It also means a set of **deeply
trained combat instincts that are wrong in BG3**, because DOS2 and BG3 share a studio but not
a ruleset. DOS2 is Larian's own system; BG3 is Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition.

The mission is therefore not "learn BG3 from zero." It is **rewire the DOS2 reflexes, then
build on the parts that transfer.** That is a different and much shorter path.

## Success looks like

- Reaching Act 3 on Balanced with the starting character, no emergency respec.
- Opening a fight by asking *"which enemy do I take out of the fight, and which save do I
  target?"* — instead of the DOS2 question *"which armour bar do I strip first?"*
- Never ending a turn with an unspent **bonus action** by accident.
- Reading a spell's tooltip and knowing, without checking anything, whether it needs
  **concentration** — and what that costs.
- Explaining out loud why a specific attack hit or missed: the d20, the modifier, the AC.
- Choosing a feat at level 4 from understanding, not from a tier list.

## Constraints

- **Difficulty: Balanced.** Forgiving enough that a merely sound build finishes the game.
  Lessons should teach *sound*, not *optimal*. Optimisation is a trap here — it is the thing
  that makes a first playthrough feel like homework.
- **Spoiler ceiling: Act 1 only.** Lessons may freely name Act 1 companions, items, and
  systems (including Withers and the respec). **Nothing from Act 2 or Act 3** — not items, not
  characters, not encounters. When a mechanic's best example lives in a later act, teach the
  mechanic with an Act 1 example and say a better one exists later.
- **Has not started playing.** Lessons can still shape the very first class pick.
- Prior CRPG fluency is high. Do not explain what a turn is, what initiative is *for*, or how
  to quicksave. Explain what is **different**.

## Out of scope

- **Min-maxing and damage-per-turn theorycraft.** Not the goal, and actively corrosive to a
  first playthrough. If a build is "sound but not optimal," that is a pass.
- **Multiclassing.** The level cap is 12, so every dipped level is a level the main class never
  gets. Single-class the first run. Revisit only if Dat asks.
- **Honour Mode / Tactician tuning.** Different game, different lessons.
- **Story, romance, and quest outcomes.** Mechanics and builds only.
- **Teaching tabletop D&D 5e for its own sake.** 5e is the substrate, and BG3 deviates from it
  in dozens of places. Teach *BG3's* rules; mention tabletop only where the difference is a
  trap (heavy armour has no Strength requirement here; initiative is a d4, not a d20).
