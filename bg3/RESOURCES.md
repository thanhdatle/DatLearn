# Baldur's Gate 3 Resources

A warning that shapes this whole list: **BG3 "knowledge" on the open web is folklore-saturated.**
Build guides state opinions as mechanics and are routinely wrong — three separate widespread
claims were falsified during research for this workspace (see [Gaps](#gaps)). Trust the wiki;
treat everything else as opinion until checked against it.

## Knowledge

- [**bg3.wiki**](https://bg3.wiki/) — the community **data-mined** wiki
  The spine of this topic. Every `MECHANIC`-badged claim in every lesson traces to it. Datamined
  from game files rather than written from memory, which is exactly why it is the only source
  trusted for numbers. Use for: any question of the form *"what does this actually do?"*
  Load-bearing pages:
  - [D&D 5e rule changes](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/D%26D_5e_rule_changes) — **read this one first.**
    The single highest-value page on the site: everything BG3 does differently from tabletop, in
    one list. Shove as a bonus action, no attunement, no spell components, potions as a bonus
    action, level cap 12.
  - [Advantage](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Advantage) ·
    [Sources of advantage](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/List_of_sources_of_advantage_and_disadvantage_on_attack_rolls)
    — the definitive list. Note what is *absent* from it (high ground, backstab).
  - [Concentration](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Concentration) ·
    [Saving throw](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Saving_throw) ·
    [Armour Class](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Armour_Class)
  - [Resting](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Resting) · [Long rest](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Long_rest) —
    settles the "am I resting too much?" anxiety. (Short answer: no.)
  - [Feats](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Feats) · [Ability score](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Ability_score) ·
    [Multiclassing](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Multiclassing)
  - [Withers](https://bg3.wiki/wiki/Withers) — the 100 gold respec. The reason build mistakes
    are not permanent.

- [**Larian Studios — official news & patch notes**](https://larian.com/news)
  Primary source, and the only place that can settle *"was this changed?"* Use for: verifying a
  mechanic hasn't shifted under a patch. Balance changes are real and guides go stale silently.

- [**Larian official forums**](https://forums.larian.com/)
  Long-form mechanics threads, occasionally with developer replies. Lower signal than the wiki
  but it is where the *why* gets argued. Use for: questions the wiki states but does not explain
  (e.g. that heavy armour carries no Strength requirement because Larian simply did not implement
  the 5e rule).

  Where build consensus actually forms — feat debates, multiclass maths, damage arguments. Use
  for: *"is this build sound?"* and for arguing with the `CONSENSUS`-badged claims in these

  Dev-run, large, live. Use for: fast answers and patch chatter.
  ⚠️ Link surfaced via search, **not fetched**.

## Gaps

Recorded honestly, because a gap silently papered over is how folklore gets manufactured.

- **Reddit could not be fetched during research** (blocked to automated requests). Every
  `CONSENSUS` badge in this workspace therefore rests on build sites and mechanics reasoning —
  **not** on an actual reading of what r/BG3Builds concluded. This is the single biggest
  weakness in the sourcing. When a lesson says "good players believe X," read it as
  *"defensible, and I could not confirm the community actually says it."*

- **No trustworthy source labels any class a beginner "trap."** Guides gesture at Monk and
  Sorcerer being knowledge-heavy, but nothing citable says so. **No "trap class" list is taught
  here**, and any guide that gives you one is inventing it.

- **Does Prone break concentration?** bg3.wiki says yes. That contradicts 5e, where it does not.
  Unresolved, and it matters: if true, the Grease and Ice surfaces a DOS2 player reaches for by
  instinct can break *your own team's* concentration. Taught with the hedge visible. **Verify
  in-game.**

- **Multiclass spell-slot rounding** — bg3.wiki uses both "rounded up" and "rounded down" on the
  same page. No number is taught until this is resolved. (Moot for now: single-class first run.)

- **Which quests are rest-sensitive** — nobody enumerates them. The wiki confirms *some* quests
  progress on rest but never lists which. Hence: "rest freely" always ships with a hedge, and
  never as "resting is free."

- **The "damage-type purity" belief from DOS2** turns out to be *contested even within DOS2*, not
  settled fact. The BG3 half (hybrid parties are fine — one AC, no split) is hard mechanics; the
  DOS2 half is folklore. Lessons say so.

- **Patch currency.** Every mechanic here reflects the live game as of research (2026-07-11).
  bg3.wiki does not version-stamp its mechanics pages, so nothing here can be pinned to a patch.
  If something reads wrong in-game, **the game is right and this workspace is stale** — tell me
  and I will fix it.
