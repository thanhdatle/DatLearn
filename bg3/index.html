Baldur's Gate 3

Same studio.
Different rulebook.

You finished Divinity: Original Sin 2, so you already know how to play a Larian game — and that is exactly the problem. DOS2 runs Larian's own armour-and-AP system. BG3 runs D&D 5e. The reflexes DOS2 rewarded are the ones BG3 punishes. This course does not teach you BG3 from zero. It deletes the four habits that will cost you, in the order they will cost you.

Lessons

  • 01

    The Armour Gate Is Gone

    There is no bar to strip. Hard control is live on turn one — and the DOS2 opening is not suboptimal here, it is backwards. ~10 min + 3 drills.
  • 02

    Three Slots, Not a Pool

    Your turn is not an AP budget. It is three boxes that never convert and never carry over — and you are about to leave one empty every round. ~10 min + 3 drills.
  • 03

    Concentration: One Spell, and They Want to Break It

    Why stacking every buff on the frontliner is not weak here — it is literally impossible. (planned)
  • 04

    The Alpha Strike DOS2 Forbade

    Individual initiative, the surprise round, and why ambush is the intended play rather than a cheese. (planned)
  • 05

    Reading a Character Sheet

    Point buy, modifiers, proficiency, AC. Build a level-1 character from understanding, not from a tier list. (planned)

Why every claim here carries a badge

BG3 advice online is folklore-saturated. Researching this course falsified three things I would otherwise have taught you confidently and wrongly: high ground gives a flat +2, not advantage; backstabbing gives nothing; and initiative is 1d4 + DEX, not a d20. So: Mechanic is datamined fact, Consensus is what good players believe, and Inference is my own reasoning. Distrust any BG3 guide that never distinguishes them — including this one, if it slips.
