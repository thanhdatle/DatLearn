The Dish Bank

Every dish, sorted into the slot it fills. This is not a recipe book — it is a menu of options per slot, so that filling tonight's grid is a scan, not a search. Times are active time at the stove, assuming the protein is already thawed and marinated.

How to read this page

Difficulty — ★ easy · ★★ moderate · ★★★ needs your full attention.
Active — needs your hands and your judgement. Only one per tray.
Passive — short, formulaic, cannot surprise you. Two of these per tray.
miền Trung Central-Vietnamese · kid-safe reliably eaten by a 3–6-year-old · weekend do not attempt this on a Tuesday.

The exclusion, and how narrow it actually is

Out: thịt kho tàu · thịt kho trứng · thịt kho tiêu — the caramel-braised pork dish itself.
In, and welcome: cá kho · tôm rim · sườn rim · sườn xào chua ngọt · gà kho. You have not lost the kho technique — you have lost one recipe. And cá kho is no consolation prize: fish is the canonical protein of the cơm–rau–cá tradition.Marinescu 2022

Canh — the soup

Children will drink a vegetable they will not chew. The canh is the highest-yield vegetable delivery mechanism you own — and in Central Vietnam it is structural besides: it is what offsets the salt of the món mặn.

Restaurant → home: 11 adaptations

Đà Nẵng's restaurant scene is built on one-dish specialists — each quán does exactly one thing, for twenty years. That is the opposite of the cơm nhà model, so it feeds your kitchen in two distinct ways: the specialist bowl replaces the three-dish structure entirely on a night you want a break, and the quán cơm plate is the weekday template.

Go and eat the benchmark before you cook the dish. You cannot reverse-engineer a taste you have never registered.

Dish Benchmark quán The home version Time Kid workaround
1 · Bánh tráng cuốn thịt heo kid-safe Quán Mậu (35 Đỗ Thúc Tịnh) — go to see the hai đầu da double-skin cut. Ẩm thực Trần (4 Lê Duẩn) with the child. The easiest restaurant dish in the city to steal. The only cooking is boiling pork. Everything else is washing and arranging. Buy rice paper + mì ướt; wash a big herb platter; mix the mắm nêm. It clears the 3-dish bar automatically, because the platter is multiple components. ~30′ Mắm nêm is the blocker, not the pork. Give the child nước mắm chua ngọt (fish sauce + sugar + lime + water, no chilli). Same meal, different saucer.
2 · Cơm gà Hội An kid-safe Cơm Gà Bà Buội — Đà Nẵng branch, 253 Hồ Nghinh. You need not drive to Hội An. A genuine one-pot. Poach the chicken → cook the rice in the poaching liquid + turmeric → shred and toss the chicken with onion, lime, rau răm. The poaching liquid is also your canh. Mostly unattended. ~45′ The best kid dish here. Plain chicken + golden rice + broth, all trivially separable. Hold the rau răm (peppery); the chilli-ginger sauce is a side, never mixed in.
3 · Bún thịt nướng kid-safe Bún Thịt Nướng – Ram Cuốn Cải, Hùng Vương Marinate the pork in the morning (lemongrass, fish sauce, sugar, garlic, shallot). Pan-sear or grill for 10 min at dinner. Everything else is raw: cold bún, herbs, pickles, peanuts. ~25′ Sweet grilled pork is universally accepted. Nước mắm chua ngọt for the child, rather than the mắm nêm-based sauce.
4 · Mì Quảng (gà) miền Trung Mì Quảng Bà Mua (95A Nguyễn Tri Phương) — taste the variants side by side. Skip the bone stock — that is the part you can cheat. Mì Quảng uses so little liquid that the flavour lives in the nước nhân: stir-fry chicken hard with củ nén / shallot / turmeric + crushed peanuts, then loosen. Buy fresh noodles. Blanch at ~60 °C, not boiling, or they go to mush.src ~40′ Very kid-friendly — in this dish the chilli is on the side anyway. Crush the peanuts fine. The sesame cracker will be their favourite part of dinner.
5 · Bún chả cá kid-safe Bún Chả Cá 109 Nguyễn Chí Thanh Buy the chả cá (steamed and fried) at the market. Then the broth is fast, because fish stock is meant to be: fish bones + shallot, ~30 min, then pumpkin, tomato, pineapple, cabbage — the Đà Nẵng signature, and the reason the broth comes out light and sweet rather than heavy. ~40′ The pumpkin and pineapple make it naturally sweet. Keep the ớt nhồi out of the pot — the stuffed chilli belongs on top, per bowl.
6 · Bánh canh (ruộng) kid-safe Bánh canh cô Cúc · Bánh canh ruộng Phương Buy the fresh bánh canh noodles. A fast fish or pork broth (~20 min), thickened slightly. Top with chả cá or quail eggs. ~30′ Thick slippery noodles plus quail eggs is essentially designed for a four-year-old.
7 · Ram cuốn cải Ram cuốn cải Đào Duy Từ (near Chợ Cồn) A genuinely Đà Nẵng-invented dish (c. 2000). Buy frozen ram / chả giò → air-fry. Wash the mustard greens, mix a nước chấm chua ngọt. Doubles as the "we have nothing planned" dinner alongside rice. ~20′ The ram: yes. The raw mustard green: no — far too bitter at 3–6. Give the child ram + rice + a mild dip; the cải is for the adults.
8 · The cơm bình dân tray kid-safe Any quán cơm — this is the template, not a dish. The default weekday structure. Rice + 1 attention dish + 1 fast stir-fry + 1 canh. Everything else on this page exists to fill it. 35–45′ This is the kid-safe baseline. Keep the canh mild; the chilli is always a separate saucer on a Vietnamese table.
9 · Bún mắm nêm Bún Mắm Bà Thuyên — kiệt K424/03 Lê Duẩn Almost no cooking: boil pork, slice. Bún + mít non + herbs + nem/chả from the market. Mắm nêm poured over and tossed. ~25′ The hardest dish here to adapt — the mắm nêm is in the bowl, not beside it. Build the child's bowl separately: same bún, same pork, same herbs, dressed with nước mắm chua ngọt. Structurally identical, mild dressing.
10 · Bánh xèo weekend Bánh Xèo Bà Dưỡng — 280/23 Hoàng Diệu, 31+ years Saturday only, and be honest about why: you need small heavy pans and a screaming-hot surface. Cooking for three, one crepe at a time, means one adult is pinned to the stove for 30 minutes while everything else goes cold. A great family activity. A terrible Tuesday. 60′+ Kids love the wrapping — as a Saturday activity. Give them the sweet peanut dip.
11 · Chả cá, made yourself weekend — Pound cá thác lác with salt, fish sauce, pepper, spring onion. Quết until it binds and turns springy — the step everyone skips, and then wonders why theirs is mush.src Steam 20 min, pan-fry half. Freezes well. weekend batch Once made, the most useful thing in your freezer: bún chả cá, bánh canh, or simply fried with rice. One weekend batch feeds three weeknights.

Two dishes to stop trying to cook

Go out and order these instead

Cao lầu — physically unreproducible outside Hội An. The noodle requires lye water burned from firewood on Cù Lao Chàm, and rice ground with water from the Bá Lễ well. Vendors who move away from Hội An report they simply cannot make it any more, and the sources are unanimous on this. It is a 40-minute drive. Do not try to be a hero.src

Ốc hút — not because it is difficult (it is a 15-minute stir-fry) but because it is a chilli-bomb unsuitable for a 3–6-year-old, it takes an hour to eat — the exact opposite of a weeknight — and purging the snails properly is miserable prep. Pure go-out-and-eat-it food.
