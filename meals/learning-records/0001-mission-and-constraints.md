# Mission set: make the 3-dish mâm cơm automatic — and the thịt kho ban is NARROW

Dat is **the cook** for a family of three in **Đà Nẵng** (himself, his wife, one child aged **3–6**).
He wants a proper **mâm cơm** — *món mặn + rau + canh + cơm*, plus a fruit or sữa chua course — on the
table **every weeknight in 45–60 minutes**, without the daily *"hôm nay ăn gì?"* spiral.

The problem is **not** a recipe deficit. It is **decision fatigue plus a timeline that doesn't
close.** That decides everything about what to teach: the spine of this workspace is **structure**
(a slot grid, a rotation, a timeline), not recipe accumulation.

## Established constraints — confirmed directly with the user

These are prior-knowledge facts, not inferences. They steer every future session.

### 1. The thịt kho ban is NARROW — and the research dossiers get this wrong

> **Banned:** `thịt kho tàu` / `thịt kho trứng` / `thịt kho tiêu` — **the caramel-braised pork dish
> itself, and only that.**
>
> **Explicitly ALLOWED (user-confirmed):** `tôm rim` · `sườn rim` · `sườn xào chua ngọt` ·
> `gà kho` · **`cá kho`**.

**This overrides the research.** The meal-structure dossier (§2.0) assumed the ban extended to the
whole *rim / khìa / kho* family of pork braises and greyed those dishes out by default. That
assumption is **wrong and must not be reinstated.** The dislike is a dislike of **one dish**, not of a
technique. `kho` and `rim` remain fully live tools on every other protein.

**Implication — and it is the emotional core of the topic:** Vietnamese cultural scholarship
(Trần Quốc Vượng, Trần Ngọc Thêm; peer-reviewed in Marinescu & Le 2022) reduces the traditional meal
to **cơm – rau – cá**. The canonical Vietnamese protein was **never braised pork — it is fish.** So a
fish-led menu is **more traditional, not less.** Teach the constraint as a *return to canon*, never as
a compromise. It is true, it is cited, and it dissolves the anxiety completely. Lesson 1 leads with
it.

### 2. Equipment — fuller than the research assumed

Rice cooker · **electric kettle** · **air fryer** · **pressure cooker** · **oven**.

The dossier flagged equipment as an open question and hedged its time-saving claims accordingly.
**It is now answered: he has all five.** Every timeline may lean on them. Consequences:

- The **kettle** goes on at T+0 alongside the rice cooker (it saves 5–7 min of dead waiting per meal).
- The **air fryer** is the weeknight workhorse — it converts an *active* dish into a *passive* one,
  which is the scarce resource under the one-active-dish rule.
- **Pressure cooker and oven are weekend tools.** Keep them off the weeknight critical path.
- The **oven** is why "baking, but only the two breakfast items" is a real scope line rather than an
  aspiration.

### 3. Time budget — 45–60 min weekdays, weekend prep explicitly allowed

The dossier's second open question ("is 45–60 min total time or stove time, and is weekend batch prep
available?") **is answered: total, door-to-table — and weekend prep is available and encouraged.**

This matters, because it makes the target comfortably achievable. The research is unambiguous that a
3-dish tray is a **30–40 minute job with prepped ingredients**. So the teaching claim is never "cook
faster." It is: **unprepped ingredients are the problem, and the weekend is where you solve them.**

### 4. The child is 3–6 — and the kid layer is never optional

- **Salt at ~50% of adult seasoning.** The reflex heuristic (PGS.TS Nguyễn Thị Lâm): *"If it tastes
  right to you, it is already too salty for the child."*
- **Do not cook two meals.** Cook under-seasoned; **adults add salt at the table.** The nước chấm bowl
  at the centre of the tray *is* a per-person seasoning dial — the format already solved this.
- **Chilli on the table, never in the pot.** Central VN already does this, so most dishes are
  kid-adaptable **by omission** rather than by re-cooking.
- **Mắm nêm needs a workaround, not an exclusion** (substitute → soften → ladder up over months).
- **The fruit choking protocol is non-negotiable:** nhãn / vải / chôm chôm **peeled, split, pitted,
  quartered**. Real Vietnamese ER cases — and it is currently July, peak season.
- **< 25 g added sugar/day.** Fruit's own sugar does not count against it.

## Implications for future sessions

- **Never re-derive the constraints from the dossiers.** They are stale on the thịt kho scope and
  hedged on equipment and time. **This record is the source of truth.**
- **Do not drift into recipe collection.** He does not need more dishes. He needs the structure that
  fires the dishes he already knows, on a Tuesday, without thinking.
- **Lesson 2 is the timeline drill.** The grid (lesson 1) tells him *what* to cook; the timeline is
  what makes it *land inside the hour*. Those two together are the whole mission.
- **The two-axis rotation is INFERENCE, not a cited fact.** No source states it as an algorithm; it is
  synthesised from how sources describe the practice. It is labelled honestly wherever it appears, and
  it must stay that way.
