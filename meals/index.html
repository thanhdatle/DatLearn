Cơm Nhà · Đà Nẵng

Fill the grid.
Don't invent dinner.

Three dishes on the table every weeknight — món mặn, rau, canh, cơm — in 45 to 60 minutes, with a fruit course after and a five-year-old who will actually eat it. The obstacle was never a shortage of recipes. It was the question "what shall I cook?", asked at 5:40pm, when it is already far too late to ask anything.

Lessons

  • 01

    The Mâm Cơm Is a Slot Grid, Not a Recipe List

    Four slots you fill, not a meal you invent — plus the rule that decides whether dinner lands in the hour: one active dish, two passive, never two active. And why cơm–rau–cá makes your no-thịt-kho constraint a return to the canon rather than a loss. ~10 min + 3 drills.
  • 02

    The Timeline Drill: T+0 → T+30

    Rice cooker on before your shoes are off. Kettle at the same moment. Stir-fry last. And the trick that collapses two slots into one pot — the water you boiled the greens in is already your canh. (planned)
  • 03

    Sunday, Twenty Minutes: Plan the Week

    The two-axis rotation as a filled grid, the shopping list falling out of it, and what to thaw when. Quên rã đông là kẻ thù của việc nấu nhanh. (planned)
  • 04

    The Mắm Nêm Ladder

    How the family eats Đà Nẵng food rather than a defanged version of it — substitute, soften, then ladder up one drop at a time. Taste is learned. There is no rush. (planned)
  • 05

    Restaurant → Home

    Eat the benchmark, then rebuild the structure. Starting with the three easiest steals in the city — bánh tráng cuốn thịt heo, cơm gà Hội An, bún chả cá. (planned)

The mission

  • ≥ 3 dishes, every dinner, in 45–60 min door-to-table.
  • Plan a whole week in 20 minutes. No 5pm spiral.
  • Weekend prep is encouraged — it is the mechanism, not a luxury.
  • A child of 3–6 eats from the same tray, safely.

Why every claim here carries a badge

The Vietnamese food web is SEO-farmed and increasingly AI-generated — research for this workspace sampled a "Vietnamese 7-day family menu" that turned out to be largely Chinese dishes. So: Sourced is traceable to a real source, Practice is what home cooks actually do, and Inference is my own synthesis — including the two-axis rotation, which no source states as an algorithm, and which is labelled as mine everywhere it appears. A good frame laundered into a false citation is how folklore gets manufactured; this workspace would rather show you the seam.
