# Cơm Nhà Resources

A warning that shapes this whole list: **the Vietnamese food web is heavily SEO-farmed and,
increasingly, AI-generated.** Research for this workspace sampled one "Vietnamese 7-day family menu"
that turned out to be largely *Chinese* dishes — mapo tofu, Sichuan, bào ngư. So the sources below
are graded, and two rules are permanent:

- **Do NOT cite `cachnau.com`, `vietgiaitri.com`,** or similar aggregator blogs. Sampling showed
  auto-syndicated / AI-generated content. They are useful only as a signal of what the genre looks
  like.
- **Discount every "TOP 10 QUÁN NGON NHẤT" superlative.** Addresses and prices in Vietnamese travel
  media are usually right; the *rankings* are partly SEO and partly affiliate. Cross-reference any
  single claim across ≥ 2 outlets. When five independently name the same quán, that quán is real.

## Knowledge

### Nutrition — the numeric backbone (highest trust)

- [**Viện Dinh dưỡng Quốc gia**](https://viendinhduong.vn/) — Vietnam's National Institute of
  Nutrition (Ministry of Health). *The* authoritative body. Its **tháp dinh dưỡng 3–5 tuổi** was built
  with WHO support on a **1,300 kcal/day** basis and is expressed in *đơn vị ăn* (serving units), so a
  parent can count servings rather than weigh food. Use for: any claim about how much a preschooler
  should actually eat. Granular breakdowns:
  [Vikinutri's đơn-vị-ăn calculation](https://www.vikinutri.com/tin-tuc/su-dung-thap-dinh-duong-de-tinh-khau-phan-an-cho-tre-mam-non) ·
  [SUA.VN's overview](https://sua.vn/kien-thuc/Gioi-Thieu-Thap-Dinh-Duong-Cho-Tre-Em-3---5-Tuoi-211.html)

- [**WHO — Sodium reduction fact sheet**](https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/sodium-reduction)
  · [WHO ELENA — sodium & blood pressure in children](https://www.who.int/tools/elena/interventions/sodium-bp-children)
  The global standard: **< 5 g salt/day for adults**, scaled *down* for children by energy
  requirement. A 3–5-year-old at ~1,300 kcal against an adult at ~2,200 → **~3 g/day**, which
  independently corroborates the Vietnamese figure. Use for: settling any salt argument.

- [**VnExpress Sức khỏe — "Trẻ em Việt ăn mặn theo khẩu vị người lớn"**](https://vnexpress.net/tre-em-viet-an-man-theo-khau-vi-nguoi-lon-4451922.html)
  Interview with **PGS.TS Nguyễn Thị Lâm** (former Deputy Director, Viện Dinh dưỡng). The age-by-age
  salt ladder, and the single most actionable line in the entire research corpus:
  *"Nếu ba mẹ nếm thấy vừa miệng tức là đã mặn đối với trẻ."* — **If it tastes right to you, it is
  already too salty for the child.** The whole kid-adaptation layer hangs on this heuristic.
  Companion: [Cách dùng gia vị cho trẻ theo từng độ tuổi](https://vnexpress.net/cach-dung-gia-vi-cho-tre-theo-tung-do-tuoi-4459846.html)

- [**Bệnh viện Nhi Trung ương — "Cách khắc phục biếng ăn ở trẻ"**](https://benhviennhitrunguong.gov.vn/cach-khac-phuc-bieng-an-o-tre.html)
  Vietnam's National Children's Hospital on picky eating, **with zero commercial agenda** — which
  matters, because most Vietnamese picky-eating articles end in a supplement pitch. The protocol:
  **cap the meal at 30 minutes**, let the child be hungry (no snacks 2–3 h before), no screens, no
  force-feeding, never food-as-reward — plus the red flags. Use for: every behavioural question at
  the table.

- [**AAP / AHA — limit children's added sugar to 6 teaspoons (≈ 25 g) per day**](https://publications.aap.org/aapnews/news/7400/AHA-Limit-children-s-sugar-consumption-to-6)
  (underlying [AHA *Circulation* scientific statement](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/cir.0000000000000439))
  The one number that governs every dessert decision in this house. **Fruit's own sugar does not
  count as added sugar** — which is exactly why the fruit-plate habit is the right backbone.

- [**Nationwide Children's / AAP policy statement on choking**](https://www.nationwidechildrens.org/newsroom/news-releases/2010/02/american-academy-of-pediatrics-releases-new-policy-statement-on-choking)
  · [CDC — choking hazards](https://www.cdc.gov/infant-toddler-nutrition/foods-and-drinks/choking-hazards.html)
  The authority for **quarter grapes under 4**. Whole grapes are the #3 cause of food-related choking
  deaths in under-5s. Use for: the fruit-prep protocol, which is not optional.

- [**Long Châu — child resuscitated after choking on a longan pit**](https://nhathuoclongchau.com.vn/bai-viet/hoc-hat-nhan-khi-dang-an-be-trai-tim-tai-toan-than-phai-cap-cuu-khan-cap-10009.html)
  · [Gia Đình Mới — hạt nhãn / hạt chôm chôm](https://giadinhmoi.vn/hoc-hat-nhan-hat-chom-chom-nhieu-tre-phai-song-thuc-vat-d9542.html)
  Vietnamese-specific, concrete, and **the reason this is not theoretical during a July fruit
  season**. Nhãn / vải / chôm chôm are *the* classic Vietnamese paediatric choking emergency. Read
  once; the protocol sticks.

### Structure & culture — why the meal is shaped the way it is

- [**Marinescu & Le Thi Kim Dung (2022) — *The Fusion Between Culinary and Communication Culture in
  Traditional Vietnamese Family***](https://doi.org/10.46324/pmp2202230)
  The only **peer-reviewed** source found on mâm cơm structure. Grounds the **cơm–rau–cá** model in
  GS. Trần Ngọc Thêm and Fukui, and documents the social/etiquette layer rigorously. Use for: the
  claim that **fish, not braised pork, is the canonical Vietnamese protein** — the fact that turns
  the no-thịt-kho constraint from a loss into a *return to canon*.

- [**Digital Dharma — "Vietnamese Nutrition Wisdom" (Cơm–Canh–Mặn–Rau: the four pillars)**](https://digitaldharma.io/hw/vietnamese-nutrition-wisdom/)
  Names the four structural slots explicitly and gives the **100–150 g of protein for the whole
  table** figure — the detail that explains why a Vietnamese meal satisfies without being meat-heavy.

### Practice — dishes, menus, technique

- [**Điện máy XANH — "7 thực đơn bữa cơm gia đình miền Trung"**](https://www.dienmayxanh.com/vao-bep/goi-y-7-thuc-don-bua-com-gia-dinh-mien-trung-de-lam-don-gian-10767)
  (+ [Bách hóa XANH — mâm cơm mùa hè miền Trung](https://www.bachhoaxanh.com/kinh-nghiem-hay/goi-y-mam-com-mua-he-mien-trung-dan-da-thanh-mat-va-dam-da-1590564)
  · [30+ mâm cơm 3 món](https://www.bachhoaxanh.com/kinh-nghiem-hay/30-mam-com-3-mon-de-nau-du-chat-cho-gia-dinh-ban-ron-1591296))
  Retailer-backed (Thế Giới Di Động), editorially staffed, and **the only major source that organises
  weekday menus by region** — including a dedicated *miền Trung* series. The best practical dish
  source for Central-Vietnam weeknight cooking. Use for: filling the slot grid.

- [**VnExpress Cooking — "Có nên tận dụng nước luộc rau?"**](https://vnexpress.net/doi-song-cooking-tan-dung-nuoc-luoc-rau-4929398.html)
  (+ [Soha's myth-bust with PGS.TS Nguyễn Duy Thịnh](https://soha.vn/thuc-hu-chuyen-vat-chanh-dam-sau-vao-canh-lam-bien-doi-duong-chat-198240718091357584.htm))
  Newspaper-of-record, bylined, expert-quoted. Establishes **the highest-leverage technique in the
  course** — *the blanching water **is** the canh* — **together with its safety caveats**, which the
  SEO blogs omit. Use for: the T+15 step of every timeline.

- [**24h — "Cơm nhà tươm tất, hấp dẫn nấu trong 30 phút nhờ 4 bí quyết"**](https://www.24h.com.vn/am-thuc/com-nha-tuom-tat-hap-dan-nau-trong-30-phut-nho-4-bi-quyet-nay-c460a1717577.html)
  Low editorial prestige, **high ethnographic value**: it prints a real working mother's actual system
  (daily greens; weekend-prep the fiddly things; portion into small boxes; lean on the air fryer and
  pressure cooker). Treat the *systems* as real and the *recipes* as loose. This is what the practice
  actually looks like, as opposed to what recipe sites say it looks like.

- [**vietnam.travel — Đà Nẵng foodie guide**](https://vietnam.travel/things-to-do/foodie-guide-da-nang)
  Vietnam's **official** national tourism board, dish by dish, with named quán and addresses. The only
  Đà Nẵng source that is both official *and* specific. Use for: what a dish is *supposed* to taste
  like, before you try to reproduce it.

- [**Hướng Nghiệp Á Âu — mì Quảng tôm thịt**](https://www.huongnghiepaau.com/mi-quang-tom-thit)
  · [Điện máy XANH — chả cá thác lác](https://www.dienmayxanh.com/vao-bep/cach-nau-bun-cha-ca-thac-lac-thom-ngon-hap-dan-de-lam-tai-nha-07017)
  Culinary-school and retailer recipes — but they carry **the technique details the lifestyle blogs
  drop**: the *nước nhân* method, the **60 °C noodle rule** (boiling water turns mì Quảng to mush),
  and the ***quết*** step — pounding fish paste until it binds and turns springy, which is the whole
  difference between real chả cá and mush.

- [**King Arthur Baking — Banana Bread**](https://www.kingarthurbaking.com/recipes/banana-bread-recipe)
  The highest-trust recipe in this workspace: professionally tested, gram-accurate, and it
  **explicitly blesses halving the sugar** (to ⅓ cup, or even 2 tbsp) without wrecking the texture.
  Use as the banana-bread canon — and do halve it.

- [**What To Cook Today — Bánh Chuối Nướng**](https://whattocooktoday.com/banh-chuoi-nuong.html)
  The best-documented Vietnamese baked banana cake found: exact temps and times, the **salt-water
  banana soak**, a real make-ahead/freezing section. Note this is a *pudding-like bread-and-coconut
  cake*, not a loaf — a genuinely different dish from the one above. **Keep both.**

- [**traicay4u — harvest table by variety and province**](http://traicay4u.vn/cac-loai-trai-cay-theo-thang/)
  The most *precise* Vietnamese seasonality source found: each variety, its home province, its
  chính vụ / trái vụ windows — rather than a vague month list. The backbone of the fruit calendar.

- [**Ahamove — Chợ đầu mối Hòa Cường guide**](https://kinhdoanh.ahamove.com/cho-dau-moi-hoa-cuong)
  The practical Đà Nẵng layer: which regions' fruit actually lands in the city's wholesale market
  (32 Lê Thanh Nghị), and when (**peak 03:00–05:00**). This is what makes the calendar locally
  actionable — Đà Nẵng buys the **union** of the Northern and Southern calendars.

- [**Tuổi Trẻ / Pháp Luật TP.HCM — "Vì sao ăn mít, sầu riêng dễ bị nóng trong người?"**](https://tuoitre.vn/plo/vi-sao-an-mit-sau-rieng-de-bi-nong-trong-nguoi-post575033.html)
  Debunks the "nóng" folk framing using the National Institute of Nutrition's position: **there are no
  physiologically "hot" fruits — the real variable is sugar.** Use for: deciding which fruits to
  *limit* rather than *ban*, without superstition.

## Wisdom (Communities)

The relevant community here is not a forum. It is **the city.** Đà Nẵng's quán are a living,
20-year-tenured body of practice, and the fastest way to learn what a dish should taste like is to go
and eat the benchmark version. **You cannot reverse-engineer a taste you have never registered.**

### Calibrate your palate — the benchmark quán

- [**Mì Quảng Bà Mua**](https://myquangbamua.com.vn/) — 95A Nguyễn Tri Phương (+ ~9 branches).
  Runs chicken / shrimp-pork / crab / beef versions side by side, so it is the one place to taste the
  *variants against each other*. Mì Quảng was listed as **National Intangible Cultural Heritage** in
  Aug 2024 ([Báo Chính phủ](https://baochinhphu.vn/mi-quang-la-di-san-van-hoa-phi-vat-the-quoc-gia-102240813112028247.htm)).
- **Bún Chả Cá 109 Nguyễn Chí Thanh** — Hải Châu. Go to register what the Đà Nẵng fish-cake broth is
  *supposed* to be: light and sweet, from **pumpkin, tomato, pineapple and cabbage cooked into it** —
  not a heavy bone broth. That sweetness is precisely what makes it a kid dish.
- **Bánh Xèo Bà Dưỡng** — 280/23 Hoàng Diệu (down an alley). 31+ years, the benchmark, a ~12-herb
  platter. Go to learn what a screaming-hot pan actually produces — and then accept that this is a
  **Saturday** dish, not a Tuesday one.
- **Quán Mậu** — 35 Đỗ Thúc Tịnh · 215 Trưng Nữ Vương. Famous for **"thịt heo hai đầu da"**, the
  double-skin cut. Go specifically to *see what the cut looks like* before asking a butcher for it.
  (**Ẩm thực Trần**, 4 Lê Duẩn, is the comfortable, family-friendly one — the better first visit
  **with the child**.)
- **Cơm Gà Bà Buội** — Hội An original (est. 1955); **Đà Nẵng branch at 253 Hồ Nghinh**, Sơn Trà.
  The reference for Hội An-style rice cooked *in the chicken poaching liquid*. You do not need to
  drive to Hội An.
- **Bún Mắm Bà Thuyên** — kiệt K424/03 Lê Duẩn, Thanh Khê. 30+ years, the city's most famous.
  Go because **mắm nêm is a taste you must calibrate against the real thing** before you can judge
  your own — and because this is the dish that most needs a kid workaround.

⚠️ **Đà Nẵng redrew its ward/district names in 2025.** The street addresses above are stable; the
ward labels in the sources may not be. **Verify on Google Maps before going.**

### Online

- [**VnExpress Cooking**](https://vnexpress.net/doi-song/cooking) — the highest-signal Vietnamese
  cooking outlet with real editorial standards. Recipes are **bylined** (e.g. Bùi Thủy), and it
  publishes myth-busting pieces **with named experts (PGS.TS)** rather than folklore. Use for: *why*,
  not just *what*.
- [**Esheep Kitchen**](https://esheepkitchen.com/) — founded by Phan Anh; detailed,
  beginner-friendly, well-photographed, strong on technique clarity. Use for: when a technique isn't
  landing and you need it explained slowly.
  ⚠️ Skews modern / international / Korean. Good teacher, **wrong syllabus** — take the method, not
  the menu.
- [**Foody.vn**](https://www.foody.vn/da-nang) — the one Vietnamese restaurant platform carrying
  **real user reviews and photos** rather than editorial copy. Use it to **verify any quán above**
  (hours, price, still open) before travelling to it.

## Gaps

Recorded honestly, because a gap papered over is how folklore gets manufactured.

- **No source gives a formal rotation *algorithm*.** The two-axis rotation (protein × technique) this
  course teaches is **synthesised** from how sources consistently *describe* the practice — it is not
  lifted from anyone's stated method. It is a good frame. It is **not a cited fact**, and the
  reference docs label it as inference rather than dressing it up.
- **The glycemic claim is second-hand.** One source argues white rice at GI ~96 falls to ~50 when
  eaten with protein and vegetables, and that drinking canh blunts it further — attributed to a
  *European Journal of Nutrition* study **whose primary citation could not be verified**.
  Directionally consistent with mainstream mixed-meal GI science. **Not taught as a number.**
- **MSG (bột ngọt) is contested.** Vietnamese paediatric guidance advises against it for young
  children; JECFA / FDA / EFSA consider it safe at normal levels. Presented as a *disagreement*,
  never as harm.
- **Vietnamese picky-eating articles are frequently a sales funnel.** Vinmec and most commercial
  nutrition sites close theirs with a zinc/lysine/multivitamin pitch. The **behavioural** advice
  matches the hospital sources and is sound; the supplement upsell is ignored, and this course does
  not launder it.
- **Prices drift.** Every đồng figure in this workspace is a 2025–26 signal, not a quote.
