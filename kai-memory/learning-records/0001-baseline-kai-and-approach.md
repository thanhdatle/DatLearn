# Baseline: who Kai is, and why the approach is a song

**Status:** active

Established at workspace creation, before any teaching. This record exists so future sessions do not
re-litigate the starting conditions or re-derive the method.

## What is established

**The learner is the parent, not the child.** The parent coaches; **Kai plays.** Kai never reads a lesson
— for him this is a song and a game. Every lesson, drill and quiz in this workspace is addressed to the
parent. Do not drift into writing *at* Kai.

**Kai is 3–4 years old.** Expected forward digit span **~3.09 at age 3, ~4.41 at age 4**; pure chunk
capacity, with rehearsal blocked, **~2–2.5 items** (R1, R2). **Backward span is below floor at 3** — he
cannot manipulate a sequence at all, only echo one. The target, `0862145286`, is **ten digits: roughly 3×
his span and ~4–5× his chunk capacity.**

**Therefore the number cannot be taught as a number.** It is not a working-memory task and must never be
run as one. It can only be acquired the way the alphabet is: as an **overlearned melodic routine,
retrieved from long-term memory as a single object.** This is the decision everything else descends from,
and it was made on evidence — R4 (preschoolers learning *telephone numbers*, sung vs spoken: the sung
group needed significantly fewer trials) and R15 (the CDC's age-4 language milestone is literally *"says
some words from a song, story, or nursery rhyme"*, while there is **no counting milestone at age 4 at
all**).

**All materials are in English** — the parent's explicit choice. The tune (Frère Jacques) is the same
melody as *Kìa Con Bướm Vàng* (R27), so Kai very likely already owns the melody regardless of the
language of the lyrics. **The tune is free; only the digits cost anything.**

**Chunking is `0862 · 145 · 286`** — three chunks, one per musical phrase, zero filler syllables. Kai
learns **exactly one chunking**, and uses it for singing *and* for speaking. The dialing rhythm
(`086 · 214 · 5286`) is for adults, keypads and ID tags only. See NOTES.md → "the chunking trap."

**Partial mastery is the on-track expectation, not a failure.** At 3–4: the song exists, he sings along,
he produces the first chunk or two on cue. Reliability converges at **5–7**. *(A synthesis, not a citation
— no pediatric or child-safety body publishes a target age. RESOURCES Gap 2.)*

**The ID tag ships in parallel, from day one.** The AAP does not recommend relying on memorization; it
recommends **name/address cards and wristbands** (R17). Memorization is not the safety net until it is
reliable — and at three, it is not. **The tag saves you on day 3; the song saves you on day 300.**

## Implications for what to teach next

- **Do not write lesson 0002 (sing → chant → speak) until Kai can sing the whole thing.** Its entire
  premise is a failure mode that only appears *after* the song is solid. Shipping it early would invite
  the parent to run the fading ladder before there is anything to fade.
- **Never propose backward recitation, letter mnemonics, memory palaces, or the major system.** These sit
  in MISSION.md's out-of-scope list for developmental reasons, not preference ones. They are not
  "advanced" — they are *unavailable* until roughly 7–9.
- **Never propose a second number** until this one is mastered. Two at once is a documented way to lose
  both.
- **The quizzes train the parent's retrieval, not Kai's.** The four drills in lesson 0001 test the
  *parent's* grasp of the protocol — when to test, which quiz form, the chunking, the stress argument.
  Keep it that way. **Kai is never quizzed by a web page.**

## Open, and consequential

Three things were **assumed, not confirmed** — and each one changes the teaching:

1. **The number has not been verified as correct or active.** Six weeks of drilling a wrong number is the
   cheapest catastrophic failure available here. Ask before the parent starts. (RESOURCES Gap 6.)
2. **What Kai calls the parent.** "Dad" is 3 syllables and an exact fit on *"Ding dang dong"*; "daddy" or
   "bố ơi" is 4 and needs the last note split in two. The song's final line is currently written for
   **"Call my dad!"**
3. **Kai's exact age.** The gap between a span of 3.09 and 4.41 is the gap between "expect two chunks" and
   "expect four." It materially changes what counts as on-track — and therefore what the parent should be
   told to celebrate rather than worry about.
