**The parent coaches; Kai plays.** This is the whole stance. Nothing here should ever be run *at* Kai as
a lesson. If a drill stops being a game, the drill is wrong — not the child.

## The golden rules — do not break these, each one is load-bearing

1. **Never test before the song is solid.** Sing it *to* him, and *with* him, for days first.
   Káldi 2025 (R10): with low initial learning, retrieval practice produced **zero** benefit
   (d = 0.034, p = .738). Quizzing early is not a head start — it is the most common and most costly
   mistake available.
2. **Cued recall with immediate feedback. Never open-ended.** Kliegl 2018 (R9): **free recall produced no
   testing effect at all** in preschoolers. Cued recall gave ~17%; **cued recall + immediate feedback gave
   ~47%.** So: *sing "oh eight six two…", pause, let him fill the next chunk, tell him instantly whether he
   was right.* **Never** "Kai, what's Daddy's number?"
3. **Always the same melody.** Wallace 1994 (R5): when the melody *varied* between verses, **sung text was
   recalled worse than spoken text.** Varying the tune is worse than not singing at all. Same tune, same
   speed, every single time.
4. **Always the same chunk boundaries.** Pause in the same places, forever. Inconsistent chunking is what
   *causes* digits to swap places. See the chunking trap below — it is the one real hazard in this design.
5. **Stop while it is still fun.** One short session a day beats four in one day (Vlach & Sandhofer, R11:
   ηp² = 0.478 — a large effect). Never drill a tired or cranky child. A bad rep buys nothing and costs the
   game.
6. **Praise per chunk, not per number.** He will not get ten digits for weeks. If praise is gated on the
   whole number he gets none — and quits. Celebrate each chunk (R21; R25: *"there's no chance of praising
   too much"*).
7. **Errorless correction.** If he can't remember, **tell him the answer and immediately try again.** Never
   let him sit in a failed guess or grind at it. **Practising an error installs the error** (R21).

## The tune — the luckiest fact in this workspace

**Frère Jacques is the same melody as "Kìa Con Bướm Vàng"** (R27, triangulated across three sources). Kai
almost certainly already knows it from nursery. **So the tune costs him nothing to learn** — 100% of his
attention goes to the digits. The lyrics are English; the melody he already owns. It is also where the
**butterfly** in the bookend hook comes from.

## ⚠️ The chunking trap — read this before changing anything

There are **two** defensible groupings of 0862145286, and the two research reports recommend *different
ones for different purposes*:

| Grouping | What it is | Who uses it |
|---|---|---|
| **0862 · 145 · 286** | The song's chunking. Fits Frère Jacques' 4-3-3 lines with **zero filler**. | **Kai.** Everything he sings *and* everything he says. |
| **086 · 214 · 5286** | The *dialing* rhythm (086 is the Viettel prefix). | **Adults.** The keypad, the ID tag, how an adult reads it back. |

**Kai learns exactly ONE chunking: `0862 · 145 · 286`.** He sings it that way and he *speaks* it that way.
Golden rule 4 is not negotiable, and it governs everything that comes out of his mouth.

**This is a deliberate deviation from the brief**, which scripted Kai as saying *"the number is 086 214
5286."* That would hand him a **second** set of chunk boundaries for the highest-stakes retrieval he will
ever attempt — the exact thing rule 4 forbids, and the documented cause of digit transposition. The digits
are identical either way, and **any adult who hears "oh-eight-six-two, one-four-five, two-eight-six" can
dial it.** So the dialing rhythm stays where it belongs: on the **tag** and on the **keypad** — both read
by an adult, not produced by a child.

If you overrule this, do it **on purpose**, and change it everywhere at once.

## The bookend hook (teach it explicitly — highest-leverage single fact)

The number **starts `086` and ends `286`.** The **"eight-six" flies away and comes back.** Tell it as a
butterfly story. Every practitioner source says the **last four digits are the hardest** (R22); this hook
converts the hardest chunk into a *callback to the easiest one*.

Also **drill the middle (`145`) hard**: `0862145286` contains **`86` twice and `2` three times**, so `145`
is the only chunk with no repeated material. It is the anchor that stops the two `86`s collapsing into each
other.

## Expectations — write these on the inside of your eyelids

- Digit span at 3 is **~3.09**; at 4, **~4.41** (R1). Pure chunk capacity is **~2–2.5** (R2).
  **Ten digits is roughly 3× his span.** It cannot be *held in mind*. It can only be *stored as a song*.
- **Partial mastery at 3–4 is the on-track result.** Three chunks of five at age 4 is **normal**, not
  failure. Reliability converges at **5–7** — a synthesis, not a citation (RESOURCES Gap 2).
- **Melody buys durability, not speed** (R7). Day 1 will not feel faster. Day 30 is the payoff. Do not read
  a slow week 1 as the method failing.
- **The ID tag is not a fallback for a lazy parent — it is the AAP's actual recommendation** (R17), and it
  ships **from day one**, in parallel. The song is the goal; the tag is the insurance.

## Open questions to ask the parent

- **Is 0862145286 correct and active?** Nobody verified it. Check **before** six weeks of drilling
  (RESOURCES Gap 6). The cheapest possible failure to avoid, and the most painful to discover late.
- **What does Kai call you** — Daddy, Bố, Ba, Papa? The song's last line is built on it and the syllable
  count changes with the answer. Currently written for **"Call my dad!"** (3 syllables — an exact fit).
  *"Dad-dy"* is 4 syllables and needs the last note split in two.
- **Exactly how old is Kai** — just-3, or nearly-5? The gap between a span of 3.09 and 4.41 is the
  difference between "expect two chunks" and "expect four."
- **Does he already know Kìa Con Bướm Vàng?** Assumed, not confirmed. If he doesn't, the tune stops being
  free and week 1 gets longer.
- **Is there a device he is motivated to unlock?** If so, the passcode trick (R21, R22) is the highest-
  leverage hack available — set it to `5286`, the hard tail. If there is no such device (likely at 3),
  **skip it** rather than manufacturing one.
- **Who is the second adult** who can play "the stranger who asks"? Retrieval must be practised with
  someone who is not the parent (R12). A trusted friend is the right person.

    ≥ 40° from all five existing topics. Nearest neighbour is llm (295°), **45° away**. Measured with the
    same WCAG/HSL arithmetic as `scripts/chip-contrast.mjs`, not eyeballed.

    what to do about that; decide it deliberately when it happens.

Per the teach skill: every option must be **the same word count and near-identical character count**, so
formatting leaks no clue. Lesson 01's four drills follow this. Note the quizzes test **the parent** — Kai is
never quizzed by a web page.
