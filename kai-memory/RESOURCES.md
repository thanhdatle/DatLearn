# Kai Memory Resources

Sources for the [mission](MISSION.md): getting **0862145286** into a 3–4-year-old, and building a
coaching loop that works on anything else afterwards.

Lessons and reference sheets cite these by tag (R1, R2, …).

**Read the Gaps section before you trust the plan.** The most important fact about this resource list
is what is *not* in it: **no study has ever taught a 10-digit number to a 3–4-year-old**, and **no
child-safety or pediatric body publishes a target age** for this. Everything here is extrapolated down
from older children, and the timelines are parent anecdotes. That is not a reason to abandon the
method — it is the reason the ID tag is mandatory.

## Knowledge

### The capacity problem — why ten digits cannot be taught as ten digits

- **R1.** Guo S, et al. **Working memory development from early childhood to adolescence using two nationally representative samples.** [PMC9618361](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9618361/)
  N = 3,562 (PSID-CDS) + N = 18,174 (ECLS). Forward digit span **3.09 at age 3, 4.41 at age 4**.
  **Backward span does not emerge until age 5** — it is *below floor* at 3.
  **Use for:** the span table, and the hard rule that Kai is never asked to say anything backwards.
  *Caveat: raw scores, not longest-span-length.*

- **R2.** Cowan N. **Working Memory Maturation.** *Perspectives on Psychological Science*, 2016 —
  [PDF](https://memory.psych.missouri.edu/assets/doc/articles/2016/cowan-pps-2016-working-memory-maturation.pdf)
  With rehearsal and chunking blocked, **pure capacity in preschoolers is ~2–2.5 items** — far below raw
  span. **Use for:** the chunk budget. It is why the song has **three** phrases, not five.
  Companion: [The magical number 4 in short-term memory](https://memory.psych.missouri.edu/assets/doc/articles/2001/cowan-bbs-2001.pdf) (2001).

- **R3.** Cowan N, et al. **With development, list recall includes more chunks, not just larger ones.** [PMC3078047](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3078047/)
  Development adds **chunk slots**, not bigger chunks. Children chunk as well as adults; they just hold
  fewer. **Use for:** why the fix is always "fewer groups," never "cram more into a group."
  *Caveat: ages 8–10. Extrapolated down.*

### Melody — the only route that fits the capacity

- **R4.** **Wolfe DE & Hom C (1993). Use of melodies as structural prompts for learning and retention of sequential verbal information by preschool students.** *Journal of Music Therapy* 30(2), 100–118 — [DOI 10.1093/jmt/30.2.100](https://doi.org/10.1093/jmt/30.2.100)
  **The closest published analogue to this exact task.** Preschoolers learned **telephone numbers**
  presented spoken or sung to a familiar melody. **The sung group needed significantly fewer trials.**
  **Use for:** the core justification for the whole method. *Caveat: 5-year-olds, ~7-digit US numbers.
  We extrapolate down one year and up three digits — the biggest single leap in this workspace.*

- **R5.** **Wallace WT (1994). Memory for music: effect of melody on recall of text.** *J Exp Psychol: LMC* 20(6), 1471–1485 — [DOI 10.1037/0278-7393.20.6.1471](https://doi.org/10.1037/0278-7393.20.6.1471)
  Four experiments. **Sung beat spoken — but only under conditions.** A **different melody per verse made
  sung text *worse* than spoken.** A **single sung exposure also lost** to spoken. Melody works by
  *chunking* phrases and fixing stress and line length.
  **Use for:** the two non-negotiable design rules — **never vary the melody**, and **repetition is what
  makes melody work at all.**

- **R6.** **Knott D & Thaut MH (2018). Musical mnemonics enhance verbal memory in typically developing children.** *Frontiers in Education* 3:31 — [full text](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/education/articles/10.3389/feduc.2018.00031/full)
  RCT (RAVLT). Sung group recalled **20% more** after interference and showed **better serial-order
  recall** — the exact skill a phone number needs. The authors name the application themselves: musical
  mnemonics for "*a home phone number or address*." **Use for:** serial-order recall. *Caveat: ages 9–11.*

- **R7.** Rainey DW & Larsen JD (2002). **The effect of familiar melodies on initial learning and long-term memory for unconnected text.** *Music Perception* 20(2) — [DOI 10.1525/mp.2002.20.2.173](https://doi.org/10.1525/mp.2002.20.2.173)
  The honest one: **no advantage in initial learning**, but the sung group needed **significantly fewer
  trials to relearn a week later.** **Use for:** setting expectations. **Melody buys durability, not
  speed.** Day 1 will not feel faster. Day 30 is the payoff. *Caveat: adult participants.*

- **R8.** **Presentation rate as a mediator of text and song recall.** *Memory & Cognition* — [Springer](https://link.springer.com/article/10.3758/BF03198404)
  **When presentation rate is equated, the sung advantage shrinks or disappears.** Much of the song's
  power is that it *forces you to go slow* in a fixed, repeating structure.
  **Use for:** two things — **sing it slowly**, and the licence for the **sing → chant → speak** ladder
  (drop the pitch, keep the rhythm).

### Practice design — the part parents get wrong

- **R9.** **Kliegl O, Abel M & Bäuml K-H (2018). A (preliminary) recipe for obtaining a testing effect in preschool children: two critical ingredients.** *Frontiers in Psychology* 9:1446 — [full text](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2018.01446/full)
  **The operating manual.** Four experiments:
  **free recall → no effect at all.** **Cued recall → ~17%.** **Cued recall + immediate feedback → ~47%.**
  **Use for:** the rule governing every drill here — **never ask "what's my number?" Sing the cue, pause,
  let him fill the gap, tell him instantly whether he was right.**
  *Note: Fritz et al. (2007), mean age **4.4**, found only ~10% — the authors attribute the gap to age.
  The effect exists at 4, but it is weaker and depends even harder on cueing and feedback.*

- **R10.** **Káldi T, Szőllősi Á & Racsmány M (2025). Multiple practice success scaffolds long-term test-enhanced learning in preschoolers.** *Child Development* 96(6), 1934–1945 — [DOI 10.1111/cdev.70018](https://doi.org/10.1111/cdev.70018)
  N = 202. The retrieval-practice effect is real in preschoolers (recall d = 0.315) — **but in Experiment 1,
  where initial learning was low, there was NO effect whatsoever (d = 0.034, p = .738).**
  The authors' own recommendation: **"ensure that new information is presented multiple times before
  transitioning to retrieval-based practices."**
  **Use for:** the week-1 rule. **Sing it TO him for days before you ever ask him anything.** Testing too
  early is not merely inefficient — it buys *zero*. *Caveat: ages 5–6.*

- **R11.** **Vlach HA & Sandhofer CM (2012). Distributing learning over time: the spacing effect in children's acquisition and generalization of science concepts.** *Child Development* — [PMC3399982](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3399982/)
  **1 lesson/day × 4 days beat 4 lessons in one day**, hugely (complex generalization ηp² = **0.478**;
  spaced 3.08 vs massed 1.75 on a 0–4 scale). Massed and clumped were indistinguishable.
  **Use for:** **one short session a day beats four sessions today.** *Caveat: ages 5–7, N = 36. The same
  authors have shown spacing effects in 3-year-olds, so the mechanism is live at Kai's age.*

- **R12.** Vlach HA & Sandhofer CM. **Developmental differences in children's context-dependent word learning.** *J Exp Child Psychol* — [ScienceDirect](https://sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0022096510001967)
  **2.5-year-olds fell apart when the test context differed from the learning context. 3-year-olds held
  up — provided they had been trained across multiple contexts.**
  **Use for:** why the number must be practised in the car, the bath, the shop, at grandma's, by different
  people. A number learned only on the sofa risks being cued only by the sofa.

### Why it must survive a *frightened* child — the mechanistic argument

- **R13.** **Vogel S & Schwabe L (2016). Learning and memory under stress: implications for the classroom.** *npj Science of Learning* 1:16011 — [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/npjscilearn201611)
  Stress "*markedly impairs memory retrieval*" and **shifts the brain from flexible, 'cognitive' learning
  toward rigid, 'habit'-like behaviour.**
  **Use for: the most important argument in this workspace.** A number stored as *a fact to recall* lives
  in the system stress switches **off**. A number stored as *an automatized song* lives in the system
  stress switches **on**. Overlearning is not perfectionism — it is the mechanism.

- **R14.** **Quesada AA, Wiemers US, Schoofs D & Wolf OT (2012). Psychosocial stress exposure impairs memory retrieval in children.** *Psychoneuroendocrinology* 37(1), 125–136 — [PubMed 21696892](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21696892/)
  Randomized, cortisol-verified (Trier Social Stress Test for Children). **Stressed children performed
  significantly worse on delayed retrieval.** The worst window is >20 min after stress onset — **exactly
  where a lost child is.** **Use for:** why "he can do it at bath time" is not the same as "he can do it
  when it matters." *Caveat: ages 8–10.*

### Developmental milestones — what a 4-year-old is actually expected to do

- **R15.** **CDC, "Learn the Signs. Act Early." — Milestones by 4 Years.** [cdc.gov](https://www.cdc.gov/act-early/milestones/4-years.html)
  Milestones are what **≥75% of children do**. At 4, the language milestone is literally
  "**Says some words from a song, story, or nursery rhyme.**" **There is no counting milestone at all.**
  **Use for:** the most reassuring fact available. A 10-digit *recall* task is far outside the age-4
  envelope; **song-fragment reproduction is squarely inside it.** The science and the milestones point at
  the same method.

- **R16.** **CDC — Milestones by 5 Years.** [cdc.gov](https://www.cdc.gov/act-early/milestones/5-years.html)
  "**Counts to 10**" is a **five**-year cognitive milestone; so is recognising simple rhymes.
  **Use for:** calibration. Kai is not yet expected to be able to *count* to ten.

### Safety guidance — the protocol the number plugs into

- **R17.** **AAP / HealthyChildren.org — "Help Prevent Your Child from Going Missing."** [healthychildren.org](https://www.healthychildren.org/English/safety-prevention/all-around/Pages/Preventing-Child-Abductions.aspx)
  **Note what it does *not* say: the AAP does not tell parents to have children memorize a phone number.**
  It recommends keeping a recent photo and, for wandering-prone children, **"name and address cards or
  identifying wristbands and medic alert tags."** It *does* recommend **rehearsing responses**.
  **Use for:** the mandatory-backup rule, and the licence to rehearse.

- **R18.** **Kidpower International — "What If I Get Lost?"** [kidpower.org](https://www.kidpower.org/library/article/getting-lost/)
  **The best source anywhere for exact scripts** — including the one thing almost nobody rehearses:
  **the *unhelpful* adult.** If the cashier says "wait in line," the child persists: **"But I'm lost!"**
  Also: interrupting a busy adult ("Excuse me, I need your help"), and **never leaving the area with
  anyone.** **Use for:** the Step 4–6 script and the persistence drill.

- **R19.** **KinderCare — "Help, I'm Lost!"** [kindercare.com](https://www.kindercare.com/content-hub/articles/2017/july/help-im-lost-how-to-teach-your-child-what-to-do-if-hes-lost)
  Quotes KinderCare Education's safety director. Source for **FREEZE**, the **duct-tape-and-Sharpie ID
  inside the shirt**, the "Helping Hands" spotting game, and the tone rule: *don't tell him the world is
  full of people who might harm him — **he may need to rely on strangers for help.***
  **Use for:** tone, the cheapest backup measure, and the mum-with-kids-first argument.

- **R20.** **San Diego Mountain Rescue Team — Hug-A-Tree and Survive.** [sdmrt.org](https://www.sdmrt.org/hug-a-tree/)
  Built in 1981 after a 9-year-old died having wandered from his campsite. **"If you are lost, hug a tree
  and stay put."** SDMRT generalises it themselves: the "tree" can be *"a large rock or bench at a park,
  **or a sales counter at a mall**."* **Use for:** Step 1. Adults search in expanding circles; a moving
  child outruns the circle forever. **Give Kai a physical object to hold** — abstract "stay put" does not
  survive panic.

- **R21.** **Parenting with ABA (Leanne Page, BCBA) — teaching your child your phone number.** [parentingwithaba.org](https://www.parentingwithaba.org/how-to-teach-your-child-an-important-safety-skill-your-phone-number/)
  **The best single source on method.** **Backward chaining**, **errorless correction** (if he can't
  remember, *tell him and immediately try again* — never let him sit in a wrong guess), praise **per
  digit**, and the rule most parents break: *"don't just stop singing once your kid learns it once."*
  Corroborated independently by a teacher on
  [BabyCenter](https://community.babycenter.com/post/a27434033/how_to_teach_phone_number) who backward-chains
  with a written model, erasing digits right-to-left. Grounded in
  [ASD Network (UNL) — Errorless Teaching](https://asdnetwork.unl.edu/sites/unl.edu.cehs.special-ed-communic-disorders.asd-network/files/2024-06/Errorless%20Teaching.pdf).
  **Use for:** the backward-chaining ladder and the correction rule.

- **R22.** **Thriving Parents (ex-1st-grade teacher) — 3 ways to teach your child your phone number.** [thrivingparents.net](https://www.thrivingparents.net/3-ways-to-teach-your-child-phone-number/)
  **The best realistic calibration available**, and it is a warning: *"Chances are **they won't be able to
  remember the whole number at age 4**... Don't be discouraged if they don't learn their phone numbers
  until they are even 6 or 7."* Also: **the last four digits take the longest.**
  **Use for:** managing expectations, and knowing which chunk to attack hardest.

- **R23.** **The Many Little Joys — teach your child to memorize a phone number.** [themanylittlejoys.com](https://themanylittlejoys.com/teach-child-memorize-phone-number/)
  Frère Jacques with a **call-and-response** structure ("I would sing the first part, then Little Man
  would repeat it back"), the **wall keypad**, and **clapping the rhythm** to teach *how many digits there
  are*. **Use for:** the echo-singing drill and the keypad game.

- **R24.** **The Cheeky Homemaker — the phone number song.** [thecheekyhomemaker.com](https://thecheekyhomemaker.com/phone-number-song/)
  The clearest Frère Jacques phrase-structure breakdown, and it ends the song with **"Green Means Go."**
  **Use for:** the green-call-button line. Via a 2nd-grade teacher (RAD Kids): *"so many kids fail at
  dialing 9-1-1 because they know the numbers, but **they forget to press the call button.**"*
  **Knowing the digits is not the same as being able to place the call.**

- **R25.** **Kalanicut — a sing-song tip for families.** [kalanicut.blogspot.com](http://kalanicut.blogspot.com/2010/05/sing-song-tip-for-families.html)
  **The closest analogue to Kai**: a **3-year-old who "can barely count to ten"** mastered all ten digits
  via a song **in a few weeks.** Also: *"Make a BIG deal about their success... there's no chance of
  praising too much."* And: **teach all ten digits, not seven.**
  **Use for:** the existence proof, and the praise rule. *Caveat: n = 1, a blog. Not evidence — hope.*

- **R26.** **Discovery Play with Littles — teach your child their phone number.** [discoveryplaywithlittles.com](https://www.discoveryplaywithlittles.com/teach-child-phone-number/)
  The **"registration desk" / doctor role-play**, where Kai must give his number to an adult who asks,
  over and over, in play. **Use for:** the single best rehearsal of the actual target behaviour —
  *speaking* the number to a person who asks. **You cannot practise this by singing to yourself.**

- **R27.** **Kìa Con Bướm Vàng = Frère Jacques.** [Wikipedia VN](https://vi.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%ACa_con_b%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Bm_v%C3%A0ng) · [Thanh Niên](https://thanhnien.vn/nguon-goc-gay-choang-cua-ca-khuc-thieu-nhi-noi-tieng-kia-con-buom-vang-1851096768.htm) · [Hợp Âm Việt](https://hopamviet.vn/chord/song/kia-con-buom-vang-are-you-sleeping/W8IUWEF9.html)
  Three independent sources: the Vietnamese children's song **Kìa Con Bướm Vàng is the Frère Jacques
  melody**, lyrics by Vũ Trọng Phương. **Use for:** the luckiest fact in this workspace — **the tune costs
  Kai nothing to learn.** He already owns it, so every scrap of his attention goes to the digits. It is
  also where the **butterfly** in the bookend hook comes from.

## Gaps

Named plainly, because the mission depends on them and none could be closed.

1. **No study has ever taught a 10-digit number to a 3–4-year-old.** R4 (Wolfe & Hom) is the nearest
   evidence in existence, and it used **5-year-olds** and **~7-digit** numbers. Extrapolating *down* one
   year and *up* three digits is the **single biggest inferential leap in this workspace.** The method is
   well-motivated. It is not proven at this age, on this length.

2. **No child-safety or pediatric organisation publishes a target age for phone-number memorization.**
   Not the AAP, not NCMEC. I looked. The **"partial at 3–4, reliable at 5–7"** expectation in MISSION.md
   is **a synthesis** of CDC milestones (R15/R16) + span norms (R1) + NCMEC's KidSmartz being scoped to
   grades K–5 — **not a quoted recommendation from anyone.** Notably, the AAP's own advice (R17) is to put
   a **card or wristband** on the child; it never mentions memorization.

3. **The timelines are parent anecdotes, not research.** "2–6 weeks" comes from ~6 blog posts (R22–R26)
   and one ex-teacher's professional calibration. **No controlled study exists** on how long a 3-year-old
   takes to learn ten digits. Treat it as a **planning number, never a promise** — and note that the most
   credible voice in that set (R22, an ex-teacher) expects a 4-year-old **not** to manage the whole number.

4. **Effect sizes are age-mismatched, and they will all be smaller at 4.** Retrieval practice (R9, R10:
   ages 5–6), spacing (R11: 5–7), stress (R14: 8–10), musical mnemonics (R6: 9–11). The *direction* of each
   effect is safe to transfer. **The magnitude is not.** Expect everything to be weaker at 3–4, and expect
   the **preconditions** — cueing, immediate feedback, enough prior exposure — to matter **more**, not less.

5. **The sing → chant → speak fading ladder is engineered, not cited.** Its components are sourced (R8's
   presentation-rate finding; R21's chaining literature), but **no source prescribes that exact ladder.**
   It is a reasoned construction, and it is labelled as one everywhere it appears.

6. **Nobody has verified that 0862145286 is correct and active.** `086` is a valid Vietnamese mobile prefix
   and the length is right — that is all that was checked. **Confirm the number is actually yours and
   actually reachable before drilling a 3-year-old on it for six weeks.** This is the cheapest possible
   failure to avoid, and it would be the most painful one to discover late.
