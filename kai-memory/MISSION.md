# Mission: Teaching Kai to remember — starting with my phone number

## Why
Kai is 3–4. If he is ever separated from me in a market, a mall, or a Tết crowd, the fastest
route back to me is a phone number in his mouth — **0862145286** — handed to a safe adult.
That is the concrete goal. The second goal is the one that outlasts it: in learning *how* to
get ten digits into a preschooler, I want a **coaching system I can reuse on anything else
Kai has to remember** — his address, his full name, a school rule, a safety step.
One number now; a method for the next ten years.

## Success looks like
- **Kai sings the whole number on cue.** Give him the first line and he finishes the song, unprompted, all ten digits.
- **Kai *says* it — slowly, digit by digit — to another adult** in role-play. Not just sings it. The song is the store; speech is the delivery.
- **Kai runs the lost-child script end to end**: freeze → shout → find a mum-with-kids → one sentence ("I'm lost, please help me find my daddy") → the number.
- **I can run the loop on new material without being told how**: encode it as a song/rhythm with ≤3 chunks → overlearn it before I ever test → cued recall with immediate feedback → space it out → vary the place, the asker, and the mood.

## Constraints
- **Kai is 3–4.** Expected forward digit span is about **3 digits**; pure chunk capacity about **2–2.5**. Ten digits is ~3× his span. **Partial mastery now is the on-track result** — reliable recall converges at **5–7**. I will not read a half-learned number as failure.
- **Five minutes a day, embedded in what we already do** — car, bath, bedtime, shoes. Never a "lesson." **Never drill when he is tired or cranky**; a bad rep buys nothing and costs the game.
- **All materials in English.** (The tune, Frère Jacques, is the same melody as *Kìa Con Bướm Vàng* — so Kai already owns the melody, whatever language it is sung in.)
- **I coach; Kai plays.** He is not a student. Every drill has to survive being a game.
- **A backup ID tag is mandatory, not optional.** The AAP recommends ID cards and wristbands and does *not* tell parents to rely on memorization. **The song is the goal; the tag is the insurance.** Memorization is not the safety net until it is reliable — and it is not reliable yet.

## Out of scope
- **Letter-based mnemonics, acronyms, memory palaces, the major system.** All need literacy and/or deliberate visuospatial strategy — evidenced from roughly age 9, not 4.
- **A second number** (anyone else's) before this one is fully mastered. Teaching two at once is a documented way to lose both.
- **Backward recitation, or "start from the middle."** Backward digit span is *below floor* at age 3 and barely emerging at 4. Asking for it teaches Kai only that he fails at this.
- **Reading and writing the digits** as a prerequisite. He can learn to *say* the number long before he can read it. Do not gate one on the other.
