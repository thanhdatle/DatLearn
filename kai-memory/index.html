Teaching a three-year-old

Ten digits,
in a song.

Kai is three. Ten digits is about three times what his mind can hold — so 0862 145 286 cannot be remembered. It has to be sung: one object, retrieved whole, the way he already sings the alphabet. This workspace teaches you how to put it there — and then how to put anything else there too.

Lessons — for the coach, not the child

  1. 01 The Phone Number Song

    Why it must be a song, the exact tune and lyrics, and the week-one rule that saves you a month. ~10 min + 4 drills.
  2. 02 From singing it to saying it

    The failure you should expect, and the sing → chant → speak ladder that fixes it. (planned — once he can sing it)
  3. 03 Rehearsing "lost" without frightening him

    Fire-drill framing, the mum-with-kids rule, and the unhelpful-adult drill. (planned)
  4. 04 Teaching Kai the next thing

    Running the loop on new material, once the number has proved it works. (planned)

The mission

  • If Kai gets lost, he can hand a safe adult my number.
  • And I gain a coaching loop that works on anything else.
  • The song is the goal. The tag is the insurance.

What's honest about this

  • No study has ever taught ten digits to a three-year-old. The nearest used five-year-olds and seven digits.
  • No organisation publishes a target age — not the AAP, not NCMEC.
  • The 2–6 week timeline is parent anecdote, not research.
  • Partial mastery at 3–4 is on track, not failure.
