# Mission: Strength & Bodybuilding — progression that lasts

## Why
I want to still be lifting, and still be strong, in twenty years. Muscle and strength are
the assets that decide how well I age, so training is a lifelong habit rather than a
project with an end date. I already have a five-day split I can sustain. What I don't have
is a **way to progress it** — right now I add weight until I miss reps, then guess. That
guessing is what stalls people and, eventually, hurts them.

## Success looks like
- Given any working set I just finished, I can decide — **add load, add reps, hold, or reset** — from a written rule rather than a feeling.
- I can rate a set in **reps in reserve (RIR)** and be within about one rep of the truth.
- I recognise a genuine stall within three weeks and run a planned reset instead of grinding into it.
- I can state my weekly **hard sets per muscle group** and keep each inside an evidence-backed range.
- **6–12 months from now:** still training five days a week, uninjured, with a training log that shows progression on squat, bench, deadlift, and overhead press.

## Constraints
- Monday–Friday, **45–60 minutes** per session, full commercial gym.
- Intermediate: 6 months–2 years of consistent lifting.
- Longevity is the goal, so **a plan that trades injury risk for a PR is a bad plan** — this rules out routinely training to failure on heavy compounds.
- I learn by doing and revisit material later, so lessons must be short, interactive, and self-contained.
- Wants online communities for form checks and programme critique.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Competitive powerlifting and meet peaking.
- Cutting/bulking nutrition protocols and calorie math.
- Performance-enhancing drugs.
- Advanced periodisation models (block, conjugate, DUP) — the rule has to work first.
