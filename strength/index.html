Strength & bodybuilding

Progression that
outlasts you.

You have a five-day split you can sustain. What decides whether you're still lifting in twenty years isn't the programme — it's the rule you use to load the bar, and whether you know when to back off. This course builds that rule first, then the volume and recovery around it.

Lessons

  1. 01 Double Progression & Reps in Reserve

    The rule that answers "what do I do with the bar next time?" ~9 min + 4 drills.
  2. 02 Counting your weekly hard sets

    How much is enough, and where the returns die off. (planned)
  3. 03 Calibrating RPE — can you tell 8 from 9?

    The skill the whole rule depends on, and how to test yourself. (planned)
  4. 04 Stalls, resets & deloads

    Backing off on purpose, before your body does it for you. (planned)
  5. 05 Training for the next twenty years

    Joint health, lift swaps, and what "sustainable" actually costs. (planned)

Sessions — one lesson per workout

The parallel stream. Where the lessons above teach how training works, these teach the movements — form, cues, what each buys you, and how it hurts you when done badly. Read the day's session before you train it.

  1. S1 Monday · Lower — squat focus

    Back squat, RDL, leg press, leg curl, calves. Knee-modified.
  2. S2 Tuesday · Upper — bench focus

    Bench, chest-supported row, incline press, pulldown, delts.
  3. S3 Wednesday · Lower — deadlift focus

    Deadlift, front squat, split squat, leg curl, abs.
  4. S4 Thursday · Upper — overhead press

    Overhead press, pull-up, DB bench, cable row, delts.
  5. S5 Friday · Arms & weak points

    Curls, close-grip bench, triceps, abs. The day that makes five sustainable.
