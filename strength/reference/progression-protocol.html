Progression Protocol

Everything needed to decide what the bar does next session. Taught in Lesson 01.

Print this and keep it in your gym bag. It fits on one page.

1 · Rate the set: RPE and RIR

RPE = 10 − RIR. Rate the last set of an exercise; that's the one that tells you the truth.

RPERIRWhat it feels likeUse it for
100Nothing left; bar stopped.Almost never
91One more, maybe. Not two.Isolation work, occasionally
82Two clean reps left; speed good.Main lifts — the cap
73Three left. Clearly submaximal.Last warm-up, deload weeks

2 · The decision table

Every main lift is programmed as 4 sets × 4–6 reps, RPE cap 8. After the session, read the row that matches and do what it says.

Last sessionMeaningNext session
All sets at 6 reps, RPE ≤ 8 Weight earned Add load, reset reps
Reps 4–5, RPE ≤ 8 Still climbing Add reps, keep load
Reps at 6, RPE 9+ Effort over cap Repeat load and reps
Below 4 reps, or RPE 10 Too heavy today Drop load, rebuild up
Identical 3 sessions running Genuine stall Reset to ~90%, rebuild

Load increments

  • Upper body (bench, overhead press): +2.5 kg
  • Lower body (squat, deadlift): +5 kg
  • Stall reset: drop to ~90% of the stalled load, then climb the range again

4 · How much of this is actually proven?

Keep these labels in mind. A protocol you trust for the wrong reason is fragile.

ClaimStatusSource
Training to failure is not required for growth Peer-reviewed Refalo 2023 (R9) — but actively contested; see below
Counting hard sets is a valid volume metric (6–20+ reps) Peer-reviewed Baz-Valle 2021 (R12)
>2 min rest maximises strength in trained lifters Peer-reviewed Grgic 2018 (R10)
No appreciable hypertrophy gain past ~90 s rest Peer-reviewed Singer 2024 (R11)
Double progression (fill reps → add load) Coaching construct Feigenbaum / Barbell Medicine (R6). No RCT found.
Three identical sessions = stall; reset to 90% Coaching construct Reasoned practice. No RCT found.
+2.5 kg / +5 kg increments Convention What the plates allow.
The contested one

Proximity to failure is the most argued-over variable in training. Refalo (2023) found failure is not superior; newer analyses suggest that, volume-equated, closer-to-failure sets grow modestly more. Both camps sit inside 1–3 RIR. Train there and you are defensible whichever way the evidence settles.

5 · Glossary

Hard set unit
A working set taken close enough to failure to drive adaptation — in practice, 1–3 RIR. Warm-ups don't count.
RIR — reps in reserve effort
How many more reps you could have completed with good form. The honest currency of effort.
RPE — rate of perceived exertion effort
The same scale, inverted: RPE = 10 − RIR. Lifters talk in RPE; the maths is easier in RIR.
Double progression rule
Advance reps to the top of a range at a fixed load, then add load and reset to the bottom of the range. Reps first, load second — never both at once.
RPE cap rule
The maximum effort permitted on a working set. Your main lifts are capped at RPE 8. A set that exceeds the cap doesn't count as earned, even if the reps were there.
Stall diagnosis
Three consecutive sessions with the same load and no rep added. Not a bad day — a signal to reset, not to push.
