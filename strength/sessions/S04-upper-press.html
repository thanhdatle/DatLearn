Session S4 · Wednesday · ~55 min

Upper — Overhead Press

Vertical push and pull. The lightest absolute loads of the week, wedged between the two heaviest days on purpose.

Yesterday you squatted. Tomorrow you deadlift. Today's main lift is the one barbell movement where the bar weight is smallest and the demand on your lower back is a matter of choice rather than necessity — which is precisely why it sits in the gap between them. That placement is deliberate: the overhead press only hurts people who lean back. Do it with a braced trunk and this session costs your lower back nothing, which it needs to be true twice over: yesterday's squat and RDL get to recover on schedule, and tomorrow's deadlift gets a set of erectors that have had 48 hours.

No bike today. Upper day. Ramp straight into the press.

Warm up

Three or four ramping sets on the overhead press. Then a couple of easy pull-ups before you add any load to them.

Press cap: RPE 7 in week one, RPE 8 in week two. Bodyweight or assisted pull-ups if you can't manage five clean reps — the 4–6 range is about the load being hard, not about hanging plates off yourself.

Why this order

Press, then pull-up: vertical push against vertical pull. Then dumbbell bench against cable row, horizontal push against horizontal pull. Then lateral raise against rear delt fly. The session is three antagonist pairs stacked heaviest to lightest, which is why every superset works and why it finishes in under an hour.

Note what is absent: no barbell row, nothing that asks your erectors to hold you upright under load. Yesterday was the squat and the Romanian deadlift; tomorrow is the deadlift. This session is the gap between them, and it only does its job if it stays a gap.

Today's calibration test — week one only

On the last set of the seated cable row

Write down your predicted reps-to-failure before the set. Then take it to genuine failure and compare. This is your second and final data point; together with Monday's lat pulldown it tells you whether your internal RPE gauge reads true.

Machine. No spotter. Nothing to drop. Never do this on the press.

The one thing to get right today

The press hurts backs, not shoulders

Everyone worries about their shoulders on the overhead press. The injury that actually happens is to the lower back, when you lean backwards to fake a vertical bar path.

Squeeze your glutes. Brace your abs. Get your head "through the window" so the bar finishes over your midfoot. If the only way you can lock out a rep is by arching your lumbar spine, that rep was too heavy — and by your own rule, an RPE you should not be at.

Check yourself

In week one of the block, what is the cap on every main lift?

Log it before you leave

Three numbers: the press load, the reps on your last set, and the RPE of that same set. Just the last set — the ordered rule reads almost everything it needs off it (§3 of the protocol shows why). The exception: if an earlier set was worse than your last, say so and give me all four.

In week one, add your predicted and actual reps from the cable row. No knee column today — nothing here loads it.
