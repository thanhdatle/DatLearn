Session S1 · Monday · ~55 min

Lower — Squat Focus

The heaviest knee-dominant work of the week, placed where you are freshest. Read this before you train it.

Monday is squat day for one reason: technique degrades with fatigue, and you have a knee history. Of the five sessions this one asks the most of your knees under the heaviest bar, so it gets the freshest body in the week. Everything else in the split is arranged around that single decision.

Read first, train second. This page teaches the session. For how each movement is performed — cues, errors, benefits, harms — follow the exercise links into the exercise library.

Warm up

Five easy minutes on a bike or rower, then ramp the squat only: empty bar, roughly 50%, 70%, and 85% of your working weight, for progressively fewer reps.

What the bike is actually for

I once told you the bike was non-negotiable knee protection. That was stronger than the evidence supports. Structured neuromuscular warm-ups reduce lower-limb injury in trials; five minutes of general cycling has no such evidence behind it, and the Cochrane review on knee exercise evaluates whole programmes rather than isolating the warm-up.

Keep doing it. It costs five minutes, it raises tissue temperature, and it makes the first ramp set feel better. Just don't believe it is armour — the ramping squat sets are the part that matters, because they warm the exact joint at the exact angles you're about to load.

The squat's RPE cap depends where you are in the block: RPE 7 in week one while you find the load, RPE 8 in week two when you run the rule. A1 and A2 are supersetted — alternate them.

Why this order

Squat first, always. It is the lift the session exists to serve and the one that punishes a tired body. The Romanian deadlift follows because it is hip-dominant: your quads recover while your hamstrings work, so the leg press still has something to push with.

Leg press and leg curl are supersetted because they oppose each other — one trains the front of the thigh, the other the back. Neither steals from the other, so 60–90 seconds of rest is enough and you buy back a quarter of an hour.1 Calves last: the smallest muscle, and nothing follows them to be compromised.

1. The rest logic is in Lesson 01 — long rest for strength on the main lift, short rest for size on accessories.

The two things to get right today

1 · Depth is a range, not a target

Squat to the deepest position that stays painless, and understand why: deep squatting is not what hurts knees. Cruciate ligament loads are highest in partial ranges and fall as you descend. A controlled full squat is not more dangerous than a quarter squat — and forcing your knees back behind your toes just moves the load onto your hips and lower back.

2 · The knee must not cave

Of everything on this page, knee valgus is the one error worth racking the bar for. Not depth. Not knee travel. If the knee collapses inward on the way up, the set is over — lower the weight and win it back with clean reps.

Two corrections to what I told you

The leg press. I said feet high on the platform reduces knee-joint load. It doesn't — a study comparing high and low placement directly found no significant difference in patellofemoral or tibiofemoral force. High placement does reduce how far the knee bends, so keep it if it feels better. It is comfort, not protection.

The two-second descent. I called it "the cheapest knee insurance available." Slow eccentrics are well evidenced for rehabilitating a diagnosed tendinopathy, not for protecting a healthy knee. Control the weight because control is how you keep the knee from caving and the pelvis from tucking — not because the tempo itself is prophylactic.

The tripwire, every set

Pain during a set stays ≤ 3/10 and dull, never sharp. Tomorrow morning you are back to your normal baseline. No swelling, no warmth. Fail any one and regress in this order: shorten the range of motion, then drop the load, then swap the movement. Not the other way round.

Check yourself

Your knee aches a clear 5/10 during the second set of leg press. Dull, not sharp. What is your first move?

Log it before you leave

Four numbers, ten seconds: the squat load, the reps on each of the four sets, the RPE of the last set, and your knee out of ten. Without them the ordered rule cannot tell you what goes on the bar next Monday, and you are back to guessing — which is the thing that brought you here.
