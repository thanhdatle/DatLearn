Session S5 · Friday · ~50 min

Arms & Weak Points

No main lift. No RPE cap to obey. The session that makes five days a week survivable rather than four days plus a slog.

Friday has no barbell to rate and no rule to run, and that is not laziness — it is design. Four days of heavy compounds have left your nervous system, your lower back, and your knees carrying fatigue into the weekend. An arms day costs almost nothing to recover from, adds the volume your biceps and triceps actually need, and lets you finish the week feeling like a lifter rather than a survivor.

Nothing here loads the knee. No bike needed, no tripwire to watch. Enjoy it.

Three supersets, biceps against triceps throughout, then abs. Nothing here needs a warm-up beyond one light set of the first curl.

Why this session is built the way it is

Every pair puts an elbow flexor opposite an elbow extensor. They cannot interfere with each other, so 60–90 seconds of rest is genuinely enough and the whole thing fits in fifty minutes.

The pairing that actually matters

Overhead extension and pushdown are both here on purpose. The pushdown never lengthens the long head of your triceps; the overhead extension does — and training a muscle at long length beats training it short. This is the one place in the entire programme where the combination of two exercises, rather than either alone, is the point.

The same logic explains the incline curl: it puts the biceps under a stretch a standing curl never reaches.

The one thing to get right today

Your elbows stay where they are

On every curl and every extension, the upper arm is fixed. The instant your elbow drifts forward on a curl, your front delt takes the load; the instant it drifts on a pushdown, the whole thing becomes a press. Fatigue makes this happen without your permission — when the elbow moves, the set is over, whatever the rep count said.

Close-grip does not mean hands together

Grip roughly shoulder width — about a fist narrower than your bench grip. Hands touching is not a closer grip; it is a wrist injury that buys you no additional triceps. Your wrists and elbows have to last twenty years too.

Cable crunch — the exception to everything

On Thursday I told you to brace a near-neutral spine under a heavy bar. Today I am telling you to round your back on purpose. Both are correct. The rectus abdominis is a trunk flexor; asking it to work while holding the spine rigid is asking a muscle not to do its job.

Curl the sternum toward the pubic bone, lock the hips, and let the abs pull you down — don't drag the rope with your arms. The number-one error is hinging at the hip instead of flexing the spine, which turns the whole thing into a hip-flexor exercise you didn't want.

Check yourself

How long do you rest between the two halves of a supersetted accessory pair?

Nothing to log — but one thing to notice

There is no main lift here, so no rule to run. What is worth noticing is how you feel walking out. If Friday leaves you wrecked, the four days before it were too heavy, and the ordered rule will tell you the same story when you rate Monday's bench. If Friday feels good, the block is working.
