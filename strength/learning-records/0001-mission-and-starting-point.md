# Mission established, and the real blocker named

Interviewed 2026-07-09. The learner is an intermediate lifter (6 months–2 years) with a full
commercial gym and a hard Monday–Friday, 45–60 minute budget. He was handed a 5-day upper/lower
split in this same session and asked for it to be taught properly.

**The non-obvious part.** Asked *why*, he did not choose "look muscular" or "hit specific numbers"
— he chose **stay healthy long-term**. That reframes the whole course. A 5-day strength +
bodybuilding split is now a *vehicle* for a longevity mission, not a performance one, which means
every ambiguous programming decision should resolve toward the option that preserves the lifter.
It is why the lessons cap main lifts at RPE 8 rather than teaching him to chase failure.

**Self-diagnosed blocker:** "I don't know how to progress." He adds weight until he misses reps,
then guesses. This set the zone of proximal development precisely — he does not need volume theory,
exercise selection, or periodisation yet. He needs a written decision rule. Lesson 01 delivers
exactly that (double progression under an RPE cap) and nothing else.

**Timeline:** 6–12 months, so there is room for several blocks and no reason to rush intensity.

**Communities:** opted in to online only. TheFitness.Wiki and the Barbell Medicine forum recorded
in RESOURCES.md; in-person coaching not proposed.

## Implications
- **Do not teach volume (lesson 02) before verifying he can rate RPE.** The entire decision rule
  reads its input from his RPE estimate. If he cannot tell RPE 8 from RPE 9, the rule is running on
  a broken sensor and lesson 03 (RPE calibration) must jump the queue. This is the single most
  important open question in NOTES.md.
- Because he was blocked by confident, unsourced internet advice, every claim taught to him is
  labelled by evidence status (peer-reviewed / coaching construct / convention). Dropping that
  labelling would reintroduce the exact failure mode that stalled him.
- No evidence yet that he has *learned* anything — this record captures the starting point and the
  stated blocker only. The next record should wait for demonstrated use of the decision table on a
  real logged session.
