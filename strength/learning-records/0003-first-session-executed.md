# First session run against the programme — completed, but unlogged

Thursday 2026-07-09. The learner ran the improvised three-day week (push Wed, legs Thu, pull Fri)
and reported the Thursday leg session **finished in ~60 minutes and went well**. Knee: **no pain,
during or after** — the first real-world confirmation that the knee modifications
(bike warm-up, painless-depth squat, high leg-press foot placement) are tolerated under load.

## What is actually known
- Session completed: back squat 4 sets, Romanian deadlift, ~60 min total.
- Duration fits the 45–60 min constraint. The programme's time budget is realistic.
- Knee passed all three tripwires: no pain during, none after, no swelling.
- Confirmed for the next block: **five days, Mon–Fri.** The three-day week was a one-off caused by
  starting mid-week, not a new constraint.

## What is NOT known — and why it matters
**No load, no per-set reps, no RPE was captured.** The learner attempted to dictate the log and it
came through garbled; I asked for the numbers and did not receive them. I deliberately did not
invent them.

The consequence is concrete: **the ordered decision rule cannot run without them.** The rule reads
its input from per-set reps and the last set's RPE. So the two-week block that follows
(`reference/two-week-block.html`) cannot prescribe kilograms — it opens with a calibration week that
*establishes* the working loads instead of assuming them.

That turned out to be the right structure anyway. See [[0002-screening-gap-and-knee-history]] — the
open question "can he actually rate RPE?" is still open, and a calibration week answers it while
producing the loads at the same time.

## A second teaching stream was requested
Same session, the learner asked for **each workout to be its own lesson**, carrying in-depth
knowledge of every exercise: form, cues, dos and don'ts, benefits, harms. This splits the workspace
into two parallel streams:

- `lessons/` — the **concept stream**. How training works (progression, RPE, volume). Rarely revisited.
- `sessions/` — the **session stream**. One lesson per workout day, teaching that day's movements.

The exercise knowledge itself lives once, in `reference/exercise-library.html`, anchored per
movement. Session lessons link to it. Five session lessons sharing one library beats five drifting
copies — the same rule `HUB.md` applies to the stylesheet.

## Implications
- **Get the log.** Ask for weight, per-set reps, and last-set RPE every session until it is habit.
  Future drills should use his real numbers, not my invented ones.
- Knee tolerance is now evidence, not assumption — but it is one session. Keep the tripwire in every
  document that prescribes lower-body load; do not quietly drop it after a few good weeks.
- Do not treat "went well" as evidence of correct loading. It is evidence of adherence and of the
  time budget, nothing more. A session that goes well at RPE 5 also "goes well."
