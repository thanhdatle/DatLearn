# Baseline: a dabbler with live money, and the arbitrage frame

Established at workspace creation, 2026-07-23, from a direct interview — not yet demonstrated
through practice.

**Prior knowledge (stated, not evidenced).** He has boosted posts and spent small amounts, and
knows UA vocabulary loosely. He has never run a campaign with attribution, an event ladder, or a
ROAS model. Treat MMP, AEO, tROAS, learning phase and impression-level revenue as genuinely new
terms on first use. He will operate Ads Manager himself — no agency, no delegation — so lessons
must carry the click-path, not only the reasoning behind it.

**The constraint that shapes everything.** $2–5k is ready and spend begins within weeks. This
makes the workspace live-fire: a lesson that ends in understanding has failed. Every lesson must
terminate in an action taken in a real account or a real spreadsheet.

**The non-obvious thing this workspace is really for.** His own adopted `docs/ua-strategy.md`
concludes ads-only LTV runs **20–40% of CPI in every tier** — meaning the honest baseline
expectation is that paid UA does *not* pay back. So the course is not "how to scale spend." It is
**"how to find out cheaply whether spending is justified, and scale only the part that is."**
Lesson 01 therefore teaches arithmetic rather than tactics: the max-CPI ceiling is the instrument
that makes the go/no-go decidable, and it must be computable cold, from his own inputs, before any
console work begins.

**Implications for sequencing.**
- Lesson 02 is competitor intelligence (Meta Ad Library, TikTok Creative Center, Google Ads
  Transparency Center) because he asked for it *and* because it is the only high-value work that is
  currently unblocked — free, no SDK, no dependency on the stalled ADR-0004 amendment.
- Lesson 03 (Phase 0 instrumentation) is blocked upstream: ADR-0004 forbids attribution SDKs. Teach
  it as specification until the amendment is recorded. **Check that status at the start of each session.**
- The moment real measured ARPDAU and retention exist, Lesson 01's calculator gets re-run with true
  inputs. That re-run *is* the go/no-go, and it deserves its own learning record.

**Evidence still owed.** Nothing here has been demonstrated yet. The first real evidence will be the
five-geo bid-ceiling table produced by Lesson 01's closing exercise — and, more tellingly, whether
he holds to those ceilings when a campaign looks exciting. Watch for that; it is the difference
between knowing the arithmetic and having the skill.
