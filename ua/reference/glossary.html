UA Glossary

The ubiquitous language for buying users. Every lesson in this topic uses these terms exactly as defined here. When a term collides with the app's own domain glossary (AndroidEmulator/CONTEXT.md), that file wins for product nouns and this one wins for media-buying nouns.

The money

CPI — cost per install
What you pay for one install. Set by auction, not by you: your bid, your creative's click-through, and how many others want the same person. The denominator of every decision in this topic.
CPM — cost per mille
Cost per thousand impressions of your ad. What the auction actually charges you; CPI is derived from it via click and install rates. Not to be confused with eCPM.
eCPM — effective cost per mille
Revenue per thousand impressions of an ad inside your app. The supply side. eCPM varies roughly 30× by country, which is why geo dominates the revenue half of the equation.
ARPDAU
Average revenue per daily active user — what one user generates on one day they open the app. For a pure-IAA app this is entirely ad revenue. The first link in the LTV chain.
LTV — lifetime value
Total revenue one install will ever generate. Always quoted with a horizon (D30, D90, D180) because "lifetime" is unmeasurable. LTV(N) = ARPDAU × cumulative active days by N.
ROAS — return on ad spend
Revenue ÷ spend, at a horizon. ROAS = LTV ÷ CPI. ROAS of 1.0 (or 100%) means you got your money back and earned nothing. D7 ROAS and D30 ROAS are the two you will live by.
Payback window
The day your cumulative revenue per install crosses your CPI. Choosing it is a financing decision, not a marketing one: a D180 payback means lending Meta your money for six months.
Margin ratio (LTV : CPI)
How much more than break-even you require. 3× is the conventional floor for sustainable growth. Your bid ceiling is LTV ÷ ratio.
IAA / IAP
In-app advertising / in-app purchase. This app is currently pure IAA: all revenue is ad impressions, which is what makes its UA maths so tight.

Measurement

MMP — mobile measurement partner
A neutral third party (AppsFlyer, Adjust) that decides which ad network gets credit for each install, and forwards in-app events back to the networks. Without one, every network claims the same install and you optimise on lies. Currently blocked by ADR-0004.
Attribution
Assigning an install to the ad that caused it. On Android this uses GAID and the Play Install Referrer — far cleaner than iOS, where SKAdNetwork and ATT apply. Android-only is a genuine advantage here.
onPaidEvent / impression-level ad revenue
AdMob's callback that fires with the revenue of each individual impression: getValueMicros(), getCurrencyCode(), getPrecisionType(). Wiring it into an MMP is what lets you optimise on revenue instead of installs. Note that test impressions from bidding sources report precision UNKNOWN and value 0.
Event ladder
The ordered chain of signals from cheap-and-frequent to rare-and-valuable: install → onboarding_complete → first_game_session → D1 return → D0/D3 ad revenue. You optimise against the deepest event that still fires often enough to teach the algorithm.
Activation event
The first in-app action that actually predicts retention. For this app it is first game session — an install that never boots a ROM is not a user.

Buying

Learning phase
The period where an ad network is still figuring out who to show your ad to. Meta's threshold is roughly 50 optimisation events per ad set per 7 days. Performance before exit is not signal.
Significant edit
A change that resets the learning phase — large budget changes, targeting changes, bid-strategy changes, creative swaps. The most expensive mistake a nervous beginner makes is editing a campaign daily and never letting it finish learning.
Learning limited
Meta's label for an ad set that cannot gather enough events per week — usually because the budget or audience is too small. It means the algorithm never got good; the numbers are noise.
AEO — app event optimisation
Optimising toward an in-app event rather than the install. The step up from install optimisation, and the first thing that requires an MMP.
VO / tROAS — value optimisation, target ROAS
Optimising toward revenue amount, not event count. The endgame for an IAA app — and unavailable until impression-level revenue is flowing. Google's own advice is to run tCPA first to establish a baseline, then move to tROAS.
tCPI / tCPA
Target cost per install / per action. You state the price you're willing to pay and the network delivers volume at roughly that cost. Where a new campaign starts.
Advantage+ / Smart+
Meta's and TikTok's automated app-campaign products. Broad targeting, algorithmic placement, minimal manual levers. The default in 2026 — the manual controls you might reach for mostly make things worse.
UAC / App campaigns
Google's app-install product, spanning Search, Play, YouTube and Display. The highest-intent Android channel — it reaches people literally searching "gba emulator" — and the one Meta and TikTok structurally cannot touch.
Broad targeting
Giving the algorithm no audience constraints and letting it find buyers. Now generally superior to hand-built interest stacks, because the model has more signal than you do.

Creative & intelligence

Hook
The first 1–2 seconds. On TikTok it decides essentially everything; most creative testing is really hook testing.
Creative fatigue
The decay in performance as your audience sees the same ad repeatedly. The reason TikTok needs 3–5 fresh creatives a week — a winner has a shelf life, not a permanent slot.
UGC-style
Creative shot to look like organic content rather than an ad. Consistently outperforms polished production on TikTok.
Spark Ads
TikTok's format for paying to boost an existing organic post — yours or a creator's. Keeps the organic engagement and social proof attached.
Ad Library / Creative Center / Transparency Center
The three free public archives of competitors' live ads — Meta, TikTok and Google respectively. Legal, primary-source competitive intelligence. See Lesson 02.
IP flag
An ad rejected for third-party intellectual property. TikTok frame-scans creatives for copyrighted content. Repeat flags escalate to account suspension — you lose the channel, not just the ad. For an emulator advertiser this is the single largest self-inflicted risk, and the reason no commercial ROM footage ever goes in a creative.

This app's specifics

Tier-1 / Tier-2 / Tier-3
Geo bands by monetisation strength. T1 = US/UK/DE/JP/AU; T3 = BR/ID/PH/MX/VN. In this strategy T3 is where you calibrate cheaply, T1 is where payback might eventually exist.
Seed game
The redistribution-clean homebrew titles the app ships (µCity, Lan Master, Alter Ego). In UA terms these are the only legally showable gameplay footage — the creative moat.
Phase 0 / 1 / 2
The strategy doc's sequence: instrument measurement (0), calibrate cheaply in T3 with $2–5k (1), scale only where payback proves out (2).
