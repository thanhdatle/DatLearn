Buying users for AndroidEmulator

Spend it,
or prove you shouldn't.

The app earns from ads alone, and the adopted strategy already says ads-only LTV is 20–40% of CPI in every tier — so the default outcome of spending the $2–5k is losing it. This workspace is not about scaling spend. It is about becoming the operator who can tell, early and cheaply, whether spending is justified at all — and who scales only the part that is.

The mission

  • Reach a defensible go/no-go on paid UA — not a burned budget.
  • Compute a bid ceiling and refuse to cross it on a good day.
  • Know when the honest answer is "stop, the economy isn't there yet."

What's honest about this

  • No emulator-specific benchmark exists. Every CPI/eCPM/retention figure here is category-level, ±50%.
  • Phase 0 is blocked. ADR-0004 forbids attribution SDKs; the MMP needs a recorded amendment first.
  • Play-policy exposure is unsettled. A takedown would vaporise the spend and the ad-account history.
  • Roughly half of IAA-only apps never close payback on Meta/TikTok. That may be our answer.
