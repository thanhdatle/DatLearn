# Mission: AdMob — mediation & UX balance

## Why
I ship mobile apps and want to lift ad revenue **without wrecking retention**. The
lever that decides both is *mediation*: how competing ad networks are made to fight
for each impression, and how ads are paced so users stay. Getting this right is the
difference between an app that earns and an app that gets uninstalled.

## Success looks like
- I can explain **waterfall vs. bidding** and say exactly when to use each, or a hybrid.
- I can set up a **mediation group** with multiple ad sources and reason about eCPM, fill rate, and latency.
- I can place ads (interstitial, rewarded, app-open, banner) at moments that earn without pushing users to churn.
- I can set **frequency caps** and measure their effect on retention, not just revenue.
- I can implement and test all of the above on **Android (Kotlin)** and **iOS (Swift)**.

## Constraints
- Two platforms to keep in sync: Android (Kotlin) + iOS (Swift).
- Prefer official Google sources over blog folklore — ad-tech advice rots fast.
- Short, hands-on lessons; I learn by doing and reviewing later.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Building my own ad server or RTB exchange.
- Deep GAM / Ad Manager enterprise features beyond what AdMob mediation needs.
- Non-Google mediation platforms (LevelPlay, MAX) except as points of comparison.
