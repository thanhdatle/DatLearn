LLM foundations

Open the box,
one stage at a time.

You use these things every day and can't say what's inside. So we build one — tokeniser, transformer, training loop, the lot — small enough to run on this Mac, complete enough that nothing is left as magic. By the end, the only thing separating your model from GLM should be a number you can calculate.

Lessons

  1. 01 A Language Model Is a Guessing Machine

    Build one by counting, and watch it generate. No maths, no PyTorch. ~10 min + 3 drills.
  2. 02 The Model Never Sees Letters

    Train a real BPE tokeniser, and watch it spell a word it has never seen. ~12 min + 3 drills.
  3. 03 How Wrong Is It? Loss and Surprise

    Score your bigram with one number, and meet the quantity all training minimises. (planned)
  4. 04 Tensors, Shapes and Autograd

    The PyTorch you actually need — and the derivative, rebuilt from nothing. (planned)
  5. 05 Embeddings — Letting Tokens Share

    Replace the count table with a learned lookup, and train it. Your first real model. (planned)
  6. 06 Attention, Spelled Out

    Break the one-token ceiling. The idea that made all of this possible. (planned)
  7. 07 A Whole Transformer, Trained on Your Mac

    Stack the blocks, run the loop, read the samples. It will babble — that's a pass. (planned)
  8. 08 Reading GLM's Report Without Flinching

    MoE, GQA, RoPE, QK-norm — which parts you built, which are genuinely new. (planned)
